Just in time! The Screen hums and the rock bounces off. Immediately I sense another movement from the other direction, and I instinctively put up another Screen to shield myself, this time without reaching my hand out, not having the time to do so.

Miss Breezy was right. It is better not to make flowery movements. Fighting is not dancing. She is holding back lesser now that I am warmed up, and the rocks are coming faster and getting bigger. The exercise is getting serious.

I block another one, back at the previous direction. They are heavier too.

“Get creative, Aesena Varinas! Try out your series of combos, different spells and maneuvers. Iacal! Iacal!”

She has laid out dozens of rocks in a wide circle with me at the centre, launching them inwards in turn. Now two more are being shot like bullets, but my eyes can catch their movement.

Just barely! “Arpica!” I extend my palms out to either side, and both rocks freeze in mid-air. Well, not literally.

“Shoosh!” I exclaim, the ice-encased rock coming in just inches from my face before it drops to the ground, this one properly frozen. Had I not-

I am still holding my hands out like a scarecrow, but the next rock is not waiting for me as it aims for my back. The Screen shines a bright blue as it materialises, cutting off the rock’s trajectory, then it blurs and fades out. I pant.

“Close one there, youngling. Do be alert. I really do not wish to be ridden with guilt. If you get hurt, we might not do this again anytime soon.” Miss Breezy warns. “This activity is because I trust your skills. Please prove me right.”

It was an absolute surprise that Miss Breezy agreed to this at all. The inspiration was from the training session that involved 'Yippa'-ing rocks at myself to practice my Screen Abilities, which by itself took me a week to get adequately good at. I just thought that it would be better exercise for my reflexes and sense of coordination if I am not the one directly ‘calling the shots’.

But the main reason is that this is fun! I also want to see if my Quick Mind potion is working properly too.

I reflect on my counters: I should not have held my hands out like that. It used too much time and it opened myself up to blind-spots. Four rocks launched in such quick succession is a new high score, however, so I am still proud. Even more so when I recovered so well from my carelessness, though I must still refrain from repeating my mistake.

Holding up a hand when casting spells seems so natural to me though. It feels so strange otherwise!

I breathe in. “Resmedella,” I whisper, newfound energy coursing through my veins. “Next wave!” I announce. It will be the tenth one.

Three stones within my vision leap at me from their static idleness. I quickly note their position before turning around, registering another two coming my way from the other direction.

There are too many rocks to focus on to cast a spell directly on each of them. My eyes and my concentration are not that refined yet. Miss Breezy must want me to fully encase myself in Screening to block them all at once, something that I took to call ‘Force-field Hemisphere’. But this session is not for testing the endurance of my Screens, it is to assess my speed and quick-thinking.

If I decide to handle them individually: I can do two rocks at most, maybe three; then the others I can use a Screen, maybe on both sides. Can I afford to try? Failing would only lead to a little head wound, or a broken arm, but it would be nothing that the Healing Spell can’t heal.

An impulsive idea arrives, and like a falling blanket it covers all other thoughts. A gut feeling comes but once, and I need to utilise it to its utmost.

I turn back to the three rocks, looking firstly at the leftmost one then the middle, calling out “Arpica!” to both of them in turn, and they stop in their tracks like flies crashing onto a spiderweb. The remaining one might come faster than the other two behind me. I have enough time.

I hope. It would definitely be a headwound if I fail – this one is about the level of my eye – but I would be granted a new skill by luck if I get it right. If I would get a headwound, then I would get a headwound; my future is set. If I would learn it, then I would learn it; my future is set!

I put up a Screen, but this time I push it forward, pushing even harder in its centre where I concentrate most of my energy onto, trying to form a tip. Like forming a pointed dent on a shield, as if an arrow just barely failed to puncture it. I realise that my arm is up again, but I do not have the attention capacity to bother for now.

The rock reaches me, and I punch it straight through with the Screen on my fist. Willed by my imagination, the Screen folds inward to wrap itself around my arm like armour, and the Sharpened tip I made shatters the rock with a loud shocking burst of sound, though I am too excited to be shocked right now. By instinct, I whip around and give the last two rocks one clean horizontal slice, and they too split into a million pieces of dust.

I stand there like that for a long breathless moment, trying to fathom all the little movements that culminated in what I just did, how it worked. The translucent blueness is still enveloping my hand, as if I just transformed my arm into a glowing blade. Sharp Abilities! It really looks like a force-field gauntlet.

I only come back to my senses after all the rocks that were so circularly aligned around me lower themselves onto the grass. I let the Sharp blink out and brace myself.

“You know, I really should be upset with you for so dangerously risking yourself like that, but I must admit…I’m very impressed,” Miss Breezy says with a hint of awe in her voice.

I feel myself beaming with relief and a sense of achievement, like some kind of heavenly warmth actually filling me up from toe to head. “Thank you, Miss Breezy,” then I look down, pressing my thumbs together, “Sorry, Miss Breezy.” Is she not going to continue this session?

“It’s alright. I just need to get used to the many more short bursts of panic attacks that I will have throughout the next few years,” she huffs, “It’s no small achievement though, child, that you could innovate on your Screen to develop your Sharp Abilities.”

“The idea came over me, so I thought to try it out. Like…just a feeling,” I tell her.

“It means that your mental and physical coordination of all that you’ve learnt is flexible, and so you can adapt however you want.” Her praises make me want more. “Making an offence weapon from a defence gear has historical background too,” she adds, “The peoples of ancient times found that iron and steel make better shields, and therefore they can be made into better swords and spears. Something like that. Screen and Sharp.”

“It feels a little scary, to be honest. My first proper weapon.” I flex my fingers.

“Then let it make you more cautious throughout your missions, that even though you can wield it, you should avoid resorting to it. After all, you’re just a child, and applying Sharp Abilities in combat can lead to deadly consequences. Yes?”

I nod solemnly. Of course I know what she is alluding to. My time-travelling duties may be dangerous and all, but there is still a strong accompaniment of adventure and wonder and bubbling joy, all which would disappear if I am faced with a trauma unfit for my age.

But to hope to never shed blood might be too wishful, and even I could not be naïve enough to believe that all my missions would be peacefully accomplished. There is a reason that there are so many books about combat and battle strategy in the library.

“Well then, shall we get back to it?” Miss Breezy asks, prompting a grin from me as I leave my thoughts.

The rocks lift up from the ground. I remanifest my Sharps, but this time on both my arms, feeling like a Wonderknight. The Sharps feel a little different from Screens in how they cling to me instead of being repelled, enveloping inwards instead of always being in a push. I think noting that difference makes it easier for me to command my Abilities.

“You don’t need to stay in one spot,” she advises, “I’m speeding up, so don’t block every rock head-on. Free up your focus. I know that you want to challenge yourself by seeing how fast you can react, but you can do so next time. Just switch to-and-fro between your Abilities and your spells for today. Test them out for fun.”

Then, just as I am getting into position, I feel it, like the entire world is being dropped onto me. But the feeling fades as quick as it surfaced. Still, I know that my energy has blinked out, and the Healing Spell can no longer do much as a remedy. The Sharps have disappeared, the fatigue too heavy to maintain them; the fatigue most probably caused by my discovering them, actually.

“Yippa.” A tiny whisper, and a small bottle flies into my hand. I drink up half of the concoction in the span of five seconds, the coolness of it slightly reinvigorating me, though not entirely nullifying the daze which is still gripping me tightly in its hold.

Miss Breezy’s presence closes in immediately. “Oh, of course. I should have seen this happening. Sharp Abilities is a harder tool compared to its defensive counterpart, and you didn’t get access to it the traditional way either.” Her tone harbours increasing worry. “Let’s adjourn. Come, child, back to the study.”

I at least have the energy for that. The green of the field turns to polished wooden flooring at the bidding of my mind, and I stumble to the big red chair at the desk, plopping myself down onto it. I squint and rub my eyes, trying to regain my bearings, then I bring the bottle back up to my mouth to continue drinking, throwing my head back to get that last drop.

“Second Wind?” She deduces.

I breathe out. “Yup. I actually made this a sweeter version of it.” It was very bitter before. The honeyhangs that I added to the recipe did not hinder the potion’s effect, I decide. After all, I am feeling less exhausted now, so that means it is working properly. Less, but I still am, though at least my head is not spinning as much as it was ten seconds ago.

‘Second Wind’. Such a cool name. Fitting too. Not exactly a huge ‘gust’ of energy, but a mere light ‘breeze’ is enough to get the gears in the human body up and running again.

“You really do like making adjustments to stuff, don’t you?” An accurate remark.

I nod with a giggle. This is actually the first time I realise that trait about myself, though maybe it is just because of recent events. “How much energy do Sharp Abilities use?” Maybe Miss Breezy put off teaching them to me because of my unreadiness.

“It depends,” she answers, “In your case, it’s because this is your first time, and you did it unconventionally. Do consult me next time, but…,” she sighs, “You’d need to practise the way you did with your Screen Abilities.”

I decide not to accept that delicious offer. “But that’s not really necessary, right? For now?” There is a reluctance in my voice that even I can hear.

“There’s only a little more than a week left before the jump, so yes, we should focus on your Screens and your other skills.”

Right, exactly, I must relieve myself of the curiosity to understand how Sharp Abilities work until everything is prepared. Though actually, my lessons have been completed far ahead of time, and we are just doing revisions. That is important too, I guess. Just in case.

A week left…My heart aches and empties, then it somehow feels as if it is shrinking into itself over and over again. I clear my head, not wanting to trouble myself with those thoughts for now. They can wait for sleep.

I stand up and swipe a hand dramatically to the left, calling the alchemy wing up. Shelves and racks lean out here and there like a folded paper unfolding, sometimes half-spitting drawers and cupboard doors before swallowing them back playfully. Liquids of as many colours as they are shades in one beautifully arrange themselves, casting an aesthetic professionalism to my desk.

“I’m in an adventurous mood for potions.” A little mind exercise will be fun, I need to wait for my energy to replenish so I can get back out on the field later anyway. “Second Wind can honestly use a buff.”

“Now, youngling, what’s exactly on your mind?” Miss Breezy inquires warily. “To concentrate the concoction? The amount and ratio of the ingredients is specifically meant to suit your age. To meddle with them could be disastrous.”

I try to structure an explanation. “In Senses Surge, the recipe has sallingsand,” I point to the grey-green powder on a rack, “which, as the main ingredient, enhances the senses. Another potion, the Higher Drive, has strength-amplification properties because of frostrings that inhibit the nerve system’s influence on the muscle-overuse and The Blood Of Vijules which temporary increases energy output,” I point to the tiny vials of golden liquid as I recall what I have read word for word. The frostrings are in one of the drawers, I think.

Miss Breezy listens intently, or as intently as I assume she is.

“Second Wind uses celanrels which readjust the soreness around the body and orange dartwings which numb that redistributed soreness. Frostrings kind of have numbing properties too, so can’t it replace the dartwings?” That is quite the structure.

She hums. “Alright. First list out the recipe for Second Wind. I’ll explain from there.”

“Um, orange dartwings, celanrels, nightglass, six-of-wendies, and quite recently, honeyhangs,” I add that last one cheekily.

“So,” she begins, “celanrels and six-of-wendies are flowers that grow in the rare wild, but usually in the same region and would not be too far from one another. Their properties complement each other in that the six-of-wendies speeds up the blood-recirculation process of the celanrels. That much you’ve read before, yes? And nightglass is the moderator.”

I nod.

Miss Breezy continues: “Orange dartwings are the natural pollinator for both celanrels and six-of-wendies. The properties of these insects hide in their thoraxes, and when crushed they mix with the flowers, with the mixing becoming much easier if there is nightglass, and altogether they make a well-rounded refreshment potion. It’s very abiding to The Law Of Nature that they work well together, and that fact also makes the creation of the recipe possible in the first place.”

I did not actually know that they are all ‘neighbour ingredients’. But it still is not an explanation for fr-

“Frostrings will inhibit the complementing process of those ingredients,” she is not finished, “I know that you wish to modify the potion such that not only would Second Wind be energizing, but it would also give your strength a boost. No can do, it will spoil the mixture instead. And even if otherwise, you cannot simply replace the dartwings with frostrings weight for weight. It will take tons of experiments.”

And I lack too much experience. But she did not say that, of course. It is a realisation I can make on my own, yet to be curious sometimes means to be overconfident, and failing to meet the expectations of curiosity can be quite the downer.

“Ah,” I try to absorb as much as I can from the explanation “I see.” I think my disappointment might be too conspicuous. Miss Breezy can catch every expression I make.

“Well…alright, let us try something,” she says “It’s called ‘Blending’, but not like squishing fruits together or mixing eyeshaders. Actually, they are a bit alike…Maybe not.” Her indecision prompts laughter from the both of us. “Anyway, you can use Magic to add the properties of one ingredient to another.”

“Like in Featheriser? And the Invisibility Potion?” One of the ingredients in Featheriser needs a spell in order to dissolve into the solution, like an oil-and-water scenario. The Invisibility Potion has a specific spell as an ingredient itself, which is quite extraordinary, and is one of the concoctions I have not brewed yet.

“Very different. I’m talking about Rune Magic. Oh,” Miss Breezy notes my widened eyes, “Oh, yes. We totally forgot. Three days ago, wasn’t it?” She laughs.

I recall that we were discussing about the workings of Conjuration Magic, how certain aspects could affect the duration that a conjuration can last and how conjurations casted by the most skilful hands could still be unstable. And yes, I remember now: it all started because I was curious as to how long the illusion of the forest could maintain itself, with its sheer size and complexity.

Then through that discussion, the term ‘Rune Magic’ popped up along with ‘Hymn Magic’, but we kept jumping from sub-topic to sub-topic and many such elaborations were put aside for a more interesting subject to be breached. My overexcitement at the things Miss Breezy were teaching me must have probably served as a contributing factor too.

“Engravings and Scripted Circles, that’s the two branches of Rune Magic. Which one does Blending need?” I think of the two as ‘symbol-drawing’ and ‘symbol-drawing in a circle’.

“The former. Now Rune Magic is different from Incantations; you can’t make your own.”

I cannot help but smile at that. “Something about the language of mags, right? The hidden language of Magic.”

“Yes, a whole other subject on its own. That lesson must wait. It’ll be a very long lesson indeed. Very complicated, though nothing you can’t understand, but…”

“We’ll focus on Blending for the moment,” I finish for her.

I sense approval from Miss Breezy. “The scroll on the second level of the first rack from the left, the green-lidded one; it has the list of potion-related runes,” she informs.

And it unfolds until its length reaches the floor. “Uppipity,” I call, letting the spell float it up into the air for me. So begins my endeavour.

Thoroughly mystified but transfixed by the intricate foreign letters, I cross from the afternoon into the evening. It is as if each of them is speaking to my eyes in magical hums and rings, the sound of it echoing in my mind, and I wonder if I am truly hearing all this, or maybe my first exposure to this ancient art might be overwhelming me.

‘Each curve its own meaning, each line as well, each dot and flick.’

And though we are not directly diving into the depths of Rune Language, the heaviness of the superficial details is already baffling me.

I sit at my desk, the scroll now not the only document floating as books like ‘Adden Relimitation’ and ‘On Runes in Potions’ join it in the air. It is the way I have studied in the library for the past few days. The big red chair can have wheels whenever I want it to have wheels, so I can conveniently roll myself back-and-forth from the alchemy wing on the left to the Magic wing on the right. Study table goals, to be honest.

On the table are dilution-flasks and beakers that are spitting vapour from their mouths and in front of them are half a dozen bottles of unusable Second Winds, failed products of my experiments with Blending. It was difficult to make the frostrings’ properties budge, and when they do at all, my attempts instead resulted in the dartwings becoming less compatible with the other ingredients.

I cannot seem to grasp the amount of energy I need to put into the Rune, and I struggled with the timing as well. Miss Breezy’s knowledge is also limited on such an experimental project, so she let me do most of the thinking, which she probably knew was what I preferred.

“You still gave it your all, child, and that should be enough for you to feel satisfied.” Miss Breezy consoles. “This is a very advanced workout.”

I huff, a little exasperated at myself. “There’s still so much to learn. I don’t mean for the jumps but…just learning in general.” I put my chin on my palms as I stare at the one rune I have been focusing all my mind on for the hours that has passed. “I’ll come back to this when I’m older, I guess.” I do not want to use too much of my precious ingredients for experiments anyway, and there might be a book somewhere on why my attempts did not work.

I am sure that I will eventually find out where I went wrong and record a more efficient method in a notebook, meaning that there should be such a book in this vast library, considering the fact that they are from the future. Also, why does this awesome storehouse of knowledge not have a catalogue? I want to ask Miss Breezy about it but…I am – I yawn – very tired. My brain is squeezed out like a crushed lime.

“Onwards to dinner, then to bed.” Miss Breezy instructs.

“But…” But such a passion for this subject might not return tomorrow. Not like passion and curiosity can do much without proficiency. But…

“We will do the combat training again tomorrow, I promise, so for that you must have a good rest.” She sounds heavenlier than ever.

And so I agree, and we take the walk out of the forest together like always.

I guess I need to deal with the lack of accomplishment from time to time, despite the high productivity of the day. It is not that I truly believe I have not accomplished much today, but I am still feeling quite deflated. However, I will succeed, either later or very later, that fact is insured at least, so all I need to do is keep trying. That, I can say, is one of the advantages only I possess.
