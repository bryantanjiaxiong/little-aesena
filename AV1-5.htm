1 - 5

The scent of blackapple tea hangs heavy in Miss Emilia’s office, keeping me attentive to the lesson.

“So that’s why they migrated. Yet even so, The Dedads control The Mouth Of Lasa, where the strait opens to before reaching the ocean, therefore the migration is further hindered. Isehr is the most strategic location in that aspect. On that note, while the island was highly sought after, The Dedads held onto it firmly against the attacks of their neighbours: the powerful Yuhenici Empire from the East and The Winterlands from the North.”

I would not be able to enjoy this as I am right now if I had not gone to Mister Martin yesterday night for a spontaneous class on Geography, which fortunately he was willing to give. The Strait Of Lasa, The Islandcity Isehr, The Heavenness Ocean and all those locations are important to learn about, since my upcoming mission is mainly taking place along them.

My upcoming ‘mission’! I cannot stop referring to it as so. I could barely sleep last night with all the excitement!

The names of the many warring factions are a little hard to memorise though, so maybe I will just try to remember as much as I can, though I do not think they hold a high necessity for now.

“The Cavals are evil! Why did they have to be so terrible?” I at least know about The Caval Kingdoms, the horse-riding peoples who raided and pillaged and sacked across what Soria was, displacing the native tribes and destroying hundred-thousands of lives, and furthering the damage when they even started fighting each other. I find myself frowning in disgust, looking at the depictions of them in an ancient artwork illustrated on a page.

On the right, the manes of horses strew about like black fire as the sharp tips of spears shine. The Caval riders are not seen in the painting, but on the left, silhouettes of the defenders cower and flee in terror, their arms flailing.

“That’s how history now remembers them, yes. But the Ripriice were a shunned race, isolated and discriminated by Mankind, and they still are. As they were hated so would they have hated,” Miss Emilia reasons, and I recall the quote: ‘Hated, they hate’. “It’s easier to just draw a line and then call it good or evil, but if it was that simple, there wouldn’t be conflict, would it? The Ripriice had to survive, and they decided that this was the best method in achieving survival. Remember, times were extremely hard then, and different.”

‘History must always be looked upon from an unbiased standpoint,’ she once said.

"Yet it wasn't only Men that they attacked," I argue, "There were Orcs and there were Yosetians and there were Elves. I mean, The Cavals were…I don't know…," I trail off.

The Ripriice needed to ensure the resources and the strategical geography of the cities and towns they attacked would not end up in enemy hands or join up with enemy forces, a situation which would threaten them, so they sacked them all before razing them to the ground. I at least know those factors, but war is ever so inhumane!

And I need to go there?

“I didn’t ask yet, young miss, but why the sudden interest in The Second Era?” She inquires, pulling me out of my thoughts, “I thought you’re more invested in The First. And why the fifth century too?”

I try to answer as quick as I can to seem as natural as possible, though the attempt might actually backfire. “I’m trying to understand more about The Great Southern Migration and its participants. I read an article about it yesterday, and I think it piqued my curiosity.” I put on a steady face.

Miss Emilia regards me with her cool emerald eyes. “Mister Martin told me about the geography you asked for, which also encompasses the area we talked about. You seem quite immersed in the subject.”

I might just blow the secret if I persist in such carelessness. “A sudden passion, and I want to make the best of it.” This is the first and last time I am using my classes to learn about specific periods and regions, even though there is still like…hundreds of periods to research on. Or I might just need to be more subtle.

Ha! I do not actually need to worry. Given my fixed future, I will not be met with too heavy a problem anyway, and even if one does find me, it would be settled in its own time and method. I need only wait! Yet…perhaps that is the one thing Miss Breezy warned me about, so I must continue to think out my problems myself regardless of the fact that they will definitely be solved.

I do not wish to lead a boring life of waiting for endings. I will experience the way to them! Though...talking about it is easier than actually doing it. The problems that await ahead…

“Are you listening, young miss?” Miss Emilia’s voice jerks me out of my thoughts. “How did the Alari win The Battle Of Glodun even though the orc-city was located on a fortified mountainside?”

Oh, um, um…“Um, they climbed up the steeps on the other side with their horses and archers,” I got this, “and rained fire onto the city from above, harassing the defenders throughout the siege. When the orcs came out of the walls, they used their horses for a downhill charge. They started upwards on…” Ah, I am lost.

“On The Foot Of Cadon.” She smiles, finishing for me. “That’s good enough. Colour me impressed; you learn lightning fast.” She heads to the bookshelves.

I beam at that, putting a hand under my cheek. Still, I cannot help but wonder…I lean forward to look at the passage. Hm, year 472, and not year 477, good. Since the journal said ‘to cross a mountain’, which I assume concerns one of the mountains along The Essarian Ridge, I would have been extremely worried if I had to go through that battle. The entry did also say ‘not dangerous at all’ though, so do I not need to worry?

Which also reminds me: what kind of ‘fun meeting’ could be waiting for me on the first jump? What does that description mean? I remember it is about going to the year 331, but without any other sidenotes. Could it be an assembly of time-travellers? I wonder if the location matters…Do I just jump to where year 331 needs me to be by just ‘jumping’? I will ask Miss Breezy tomorrow; best not wander directionless in this lane of thought.

Back to the subject of year 477, I have been pondering on the possibilities all night, but the reason I need to travel to Isehr all the way from Askenny has yet to properly dawn on me. Though…I could be participating in one of the migrations, most probably actually, since ‘Many carriages’ is written in that entry. But still, why is a little girl needed there?

A better question is: why is a little girl needed anywhere?

If the purpose of my first ‘mission’ actually is participating in a migration, the entry could have at least stated the details of that migration so I could at least look for information on it. But alas…Maybe the purpose is not a migration? A trade caravan expedition, maybe? An…An army, perhaps? Whatever.

Miss Emilia bends over the desk, opening another book in front of me. “Here, this perfectly illustrates and analyses the spread of The Cavals from Eastern Wholeone, how they are divided by their own ever-in-conflict leaders,” she flips and points and flips, “how they affect their neighbours, which eventually also became their targets of conquests, and how they altogether ultimately met their fall.”

Gold! I almost voiced that out loud. I love mapbooks! The word ‘Askenny’ immediately captures my eye, the ancient version of Ashcurn. Though the names are quite similar themselves, I still double-checked with my phone yesterday night, for assurance.

I wriggle in my seat to readjust the folds of my dress, then I start flipping through the book, putting my focus on the region. Askenny changed hands a few times: from a self-governing Yosetian-city at first, till it became a Yuhenici settlement, then it was raided by The White Ripriice, but after that it was rebuilt, yet it was raided once more, this time losing many lives…

I turn to the last few pages, but the city remains uncoloured and unmarked by any of the faction names, meaning that it had disappeared from the world, probably destroyed. I did also read that it was only rebuilt two or three hundred years ago, with the new settlers finding only ash and dust on their arrival, and maybe that was where the city got the new name.

I go back to the front. “Where did the Yosetians move to?” I go on flipping through the book again, watching as the red colour that indicates their faction ownership shrinks into a speck on the map before vanishing completely.

“They no longer have a state of their own, having gone northward into The Empire, integrating into Yuhenici society and mingling with other races, mainly Men,” Miss Emilia explains.

“‘Have’? Not ‘had’?” I notice. I always thought they were wiped out cleanly, and I have always felt sad for them.

She hums. “It’s still a controversial subject. Redskins are no longer seen, but Fire Abilities live on in their descendants. Some Yosetian traits remain while others disappear into time. It’s a rather dark history.”

Mankind has always loved boasting, it is almost like a subtle tradition to boast, and I think they would prefer it to be said that they ‘absorbed’ the Abilities of other races. There was a children’s story, I remember…‘The Thieves Of Gifts’, and it described a similar plot which I only understood better after looking into a more mature version.

The Yosetians’ cultures were stolen by Men, as were others like the Varyaeyi and the Gusts. Sometimes the history of Mankind makes me feel shameful of being one. What horror…But…

Fire Abilities! Fire Magic! I might learn that soon! Yet…

“Only those of Yosetian-descent have access to Fire Abilities, right?” What did Miss Breezy mean when she said I will practice Magic with her, even though she must have known I only have Man-blood. I mean, as far as I know. Maybe there is a kind of Magic to learn the Magic I normally would not be able to. Or maybe she simply meant Sharp and Screen Magic, the custom Abilities borne by Men.

“That’s correct. I have to leave you now, young miss, but feel free to use the room as long as you like.” Miss Emilia holds the door open.

I look up from the book. “Are you going to the aviary?”

“Yes. Oh, last…week, you wished to tend to the plushes when they hatched, I recall. Do you still-” She laughs upon noticing the look in my eyes, “Well, we have five newborn plushes this morning. Come to them after you finish up here. I will ready them for you in an hour.”

“Thank you, Miss Emilia,” I say as she leaves.

Then the door reopens. “I’ll let you name one,” she offers excitedly.

I open my mouth after registering what she said but the door closes just as quickly. I take in a long breath instead, filling my lungs with the aroma of blackapple.

Sharp Magic. Screen Magic. If I assume correctly, Miss Alice will be the one to teach them to me in the far future. But she would probably not consent to let a twelve-year old practice Magic, unless I try to explain everything to her. There is also the necessary application procedure to be a certified Abilitier.

No, I cannot. Miss Breezy is the only one that can teach me Magic for now. No one else can know. Though…what harm can it be, if the future is already set and I am ‘doomed’ to succeed? I guess most important decisions will be based on my personality, therefore I should be loyal to it and not tell anyone about this ‘secret’ if I truly feel that I should be cautious.

Ah. Anyever will do. I keep thinking that my actions might change the course of time before remembering that they cannot, and when I do remember the latter, I want to try to change the course of time, though, again, I am also aware that I cannot.

I find myself staring at the first page of the mapbook, realizing that I have probably been doing that for a couple minutes. I flip through the thickness casually, squeezing my mind in my attempted memorization of as many names as possible. Then I heave my head back, sighing deeply.

Miss Alice has a session with me later: Magical Science! I will get to learn how Magic makes sense and how most often it does not, and how there is a way of understanding in even the most unexplainable cases.

I am actually relieved that it will be The Second Era that I am jumping to, and not The First, which had too many irregularities to consider. Magic all around the world was ridiculously unstable back then. Sometimes mountains could move or disappear, and sometimes the sky could come crashing down, and sometimes the air could compress to squeeze everything like it is wringing out a sponge.

The Magical Happenings themselves are interesting to read about, but it must have been harrowing for everyone in that era, when technology and knowledge were not yet too advanced and the chaos was further intensified by war and famine. At least, that is the extent of what I learnt from books.

The beginning of The Second Era had The Shifted Heavens Phase, which saw a permanent halt to many Magical Happenings. There are a few exceptions, no doubt, as most Magical things usually have. I think there were two or ten Magical portals that linked places that would otherwise be far apart; Miss Emilia mentioned it just now when we were going through some unorthodox troop-travelling methods of the period, but the names of the factions very easily escaped my memory. And of course, there are still remaining instances of my favourite Magical Happening: Water Rings, massive and awesome sinkholes in bodies of water.

Concerning The Shifted Heavens Phase is a most famous story: The Legend Of The Five Heroes is known by every child and adult of every race. I look to the bookshelves to see if Miss Emilia has a version of it, but there is not a similar title.

She has a huge collection of books, not comparable to the treasure vault that is…my…study, but still altogether impressive. The ones on the middle-left shelf are actually written by her own mind and hand, with titles like ‘An Analysis of The Spread of Anglish’ and ‘The Current House Of The Usii, Descendants Of Yehova’. I take a closer look, rolling my chair to it. There is an especially thick volume titled ‘Thellioni and Lysisa’, the two goddesses of ancient Harmonia, a First Era faction.

I admire Miss Emilia’s dedication to her passion. Everyone else in the manor is the same as well, with their own hobbies and interests. It feels like an accomplished university where I am the only student.

Staring at ‘Thellioni and Lysisa’, I hop back into my thoughts. I will still have to visit The First Era despite all its terrors. There were a few First Era entries in the journal. It might just be my very next one.

How did things come to today again? I do not remember. The blue light…I take out the pebble from my pocket, observing its uneven surfaces. Is this truly not a dream? Is this all really real?

Like, really really?

I would be disappointed if it is not, but I might actually be relieved, knowing that my life is going to be the same even though I am ever yearning for something new and different. The first ‘jump’ would lead to a completely new life as an actual time-traveller. No more twelve-year old girl with a normal twelve-year old life.

It must make sense in the end, that I am the one to do this and not anyone else. It has to, and I am destined to find out.

Ah, yes! I am ‘finding out’. It does not truly matter if I know my ending, but along the way towards it, it matters that I must find the meaning of it. Why and how, and then why and how. That is a great thought to have, I think.

I kick my legs out to let the chair roll around, its wheels silent, and I spin. The dark red ceiling above looks familiarly warming even though I only came to know it two weeks ago.

I really want to discuss this with someone. Well, someone other than Miss Breezy. Someone that can be amazed by the wonder of it all. I want to tell Grandmama, and I want to send a call to Mama and Dada. I wish to see their reactions to this wonderfully incredulous tale.

Hm…The sunrays shining in get caught in my eye as my spinning nears the window, and I stop. Miss Emilia’s study overlooks the gemlilies, and if I press my face to the glass, I could see the windows of Mister Martin’s. I squint, my eyes catching the outline of a petalfly on the cover of an upright book, stood up on the wall-shelf. A petalfly…or a rose, whatever the flower is. That must be his latest Gardening Journal.

I push away, continuing my ponderations, but…I will decide after my first jump, I guess. Maybe I might reveal everything. Maybe I will not. One thing I know for sure is that this month holds a promising amount of adventures and exploits.

And I can’t sit still thinking about it!
