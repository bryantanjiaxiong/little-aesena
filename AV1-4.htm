Ay’on’igh’ro’by

I dance and twirl with the song in my head. The sun is surprisingly calm today despite its early glare, bright but not too warm, entertaining my morning stroll, and so I thank my luck.

“Jolly mood today, Aesena Varinas,” Miss Breezy remarks, announcing her presence before I could even think to wait for her outside the woods. She was the one waiting instead.

“I was jolly yesterday!” I remind her.

She laughs lightly. “Jollier now. Good morning.”

“Good morning, Miss Breezy. Shall we?” I skip into the forest, with Miss Breezy later redirecting me a few steps to the right.

The canopy appears to be healthier in a way too, like my mood, and I truly feel that this day is blessed. There is still no chirping of birds or cricking of insects as would have been easily heard in normal woods, however. I wonder if my mind made up the chirps yesterday. A short while later, I am biting on my fifth sandwich as I circle the log and the rock for probably the twentieth time.

“Everyone said that the woods are dangerous, and that I shouldn’t go near them, but Grandmama gave me a hint of her approval, as if she knew that I went for the light.” I continue telling Miss Breezy about last night’s dinner between chews.

“Hm? What did she say?”

“She said she would have done it, and well, she did tell me to go for an adventure. Anyway, why do you think they said it’s dangerous?” Miss Alice or Miss Emilia must surely have ventured into these woods before, only to have found nothing interesting, save for the tall too-neatly-arranged trees and the absence of anything else, both which…are actually very peculiar.

“They probably misunderstood the woods you are referring to. Though…you see, at the entrance, ten-or-so more steps to the right or left and the woods might have been dangerous,” Miss Breezy reveals. “And though once inside, these woods may seem endless, it actually is, because it’s separated from your plane of reality.”

‘Plane of reality’? I almost laugh. The way she said it made it sound like it came out of a movie. But…“Yes, I’ve actually thought about that possibility last night. Fascinating!” This is almost like a different dimension, if it is not one. Exhilarating! “That would mean that I could have gone really lost yesterday,” I note.

“I would have come for you if it had looked hopeless that you would find your own way, or if you’d started to cry. But you’re smart.” Breezy is making me feel observed again.

“Is that one of the sides to your coin?” I can feel her shrugging to that. “So the blue light is intended for the person seeking it to come to it in one direction from the spot of my tree…”

“Coincidentally.”

I stop pacing. “Or not. Miss Breezy, I’ve been thinking about it, but all of this somehow feels planned. Truly.” I gesture around the clearing with my sandwich.

“Does it now?”

I stay quiet for two moments, musing on the situation. “What’re you wanting to show me, by the way?”

She ‘breezes’ around me then comes to a halt by my side. “To be extremely honest with you, I’m quite nervous about today.”

‘Nervous’? “So…” I trail off in confusion. “Are you going to let me see it?” I ask, a little impatiently, then trying to compensate: “Or not?” I add.

“Ha. It’s meant to be anyway. Fine, come,” she urges, but I do not understand her first line. “Hold out your hand to the log and the rock, Aesena Varinas, and imagine them vanishing. In the same time, picture the woods shrinking into this clearing.” Those are quite a few instructions to take in so quickly, but she is not finished: “Imagine the sunrays melting with the leaves and the trunks of the trees, and the green of the grass and the blue of the sky is woven together.”

I look at the unfinished sandwich in my hand, and she laughs upon noticing, a bright giggle sounding around me.

“I apologise. I got too excited. Take your time, child, and I’ll wait,” she says.

I quickly pop the bread into my mouth, chew as quickly, and swallow it. “Alright, can you repeat what you said?” I am so ready for something new.

“Hold a hand out.”

And I do.

“Towards the log and the rock and think about how they could possibly disappear into thin air. Everything around you swirls into a single centre, and you’re in somewhere else.”

‘Somewhere else’?

And I do. In my hurry, it is hard to imagine anything. Then suddenly I remember my basket sitting on the ground before deciding that I should get this over with first then maybe I can get to the basket later. I focus on the green and the blue and the grey and the yellow and the brown, and the green and the grey and the bl-

Woah!

I almost tripped backwards. The trees are gone and the canopy is gone and the clearing is gone, and replacing them is a large room, with shelves of books lining the walls around me, reaching up to the height of where the canopy roof was. Replacing the spot where the log and the rock was is a long desk with balls of light hovering above and around it. On it are piles of books, and also scrolls and loose stacks of paper. In front of the desk is a big red chair.

I am frozen, I realise, in disbelief, so I loosen myself up. However…woah. I did expect being brought to some other place, but like, with butterflies and singing flowers, or a meadow with dancing animals. This, I did not expect.

Floating below the ceiling, which is the internal surface of a dome, are more lights of varying bright colours. The ones at the desk are only a dim yellow. As I squint harder, the spiral carvings etched onto the ceiling becomes clearer, constantly shifting as if the ceiling is made of panels of display screens. But they are not, for I know it is Magic.

“That was fast,” Miss Breezy remarks in a giggly shock, and I turn to her voice.

Incredible! “This is incredible! Where am I?” The books are the main attraction. The room is sided by six walls, I count, making an equilateral hexagon, I think, and the wallpaper might as well be the bindings of the books, as there is simply no gap between the shelves. Some at the higher levels are not as filled as the ones below them, I notice, with books that are slanting and stacking up steeply. I start walking to one of the shelf-walls.

“Careful!”

I pause at Miss Breezy’s warning, looking around, then down at the picnic basket I almost kicked. Ha! It did follow me here after all, providing me with one more factor to consider later when I am contemplating this…wondrous Magic.

The floor is made of polished wood, reflecting the lights so high above it. A myriad of patterns swirls on, or within, the tiles, as if there is a river in each of them. It is mesmerizing. I jump from tile to tile, watching as the swirls disperse beneath my steps before reconverging where my foot leaves the surface.

Approaching the books, I find that they are not at all dusty, though how could they be? There are no windows in sight, but the room seems well-ventilated. Magic! No doubt. But this is not the Magic I frequently heard nor know about.

The woods I could deem as a Magical Happening, but the Magic in this room seems more casual, and artificial, like the work of a wizard or a witch. Maybe it does hide an artifact that an evil sorcerer desires.

I reach for a book, bringing it off the shelf. The cover reads: ‘Red Series: Caves Of Enbad’. I put it back, taking out another: ‘Year-dawn Stars’. It is a little inconvenient, I opine, how the bindings themselves do not display the title. It must be difficult for the owner of this place to find a specific book, though maybe, again, Magic could easily solve that inconvenience.

I look for Miss Breezy, though of course I could not actually do that. “Miss Breezy, where am I?”

“Hm…Well…How do I put it?” She mutters. “This is your study,” she finally says.

I squint in confusion, not sure if I heard that right. “Come again?”

“I did tell you that things will be too much for you to take in too quickly. But let’s begin: all of this is meant for you. Everything here.” Her voice bounces of the shelf-walls of the room, resonating though not producing notably echoey repetition.

‘Everything here’? “Everything here?” I look around again. “Prepared by whom?”

“All the answers must come in a particular order, to minimise the confusion. The next thing I am supposed to tell you is: you’ll be learning all kinds of Magic here, as you need to.”

“‘Supposed to-’, you’re supposed to tell me things? Wha-…I need to learn Magic? Here…Like here here?” That explains the books, though they do not look to have content about Magic. ‘Caves’ and ‘Stars’…Well, there could be extra details…I cannot be sure. There are too many questions to ask, so many such that waiting for Miss Breezy to slowly explain might be the best choice to go down. “I’m only like…twelve,” I add. Things are happening too fast.

“Best period to learn anything. Going on: you are a key figure in setting in motion things that have already been set in motion.” Miss Breezy has a hint of reluctance in this delivery, her voice faltering.

I cannot help squinting again, this time also tilting my head. “What does that mean?”

“You’re needed to make things that have already happened happen,” she clarifies, though she actually just paraphrased what she previously said.

I understood the first time, of course. “Time-travelling?” It is just getting a little bit too unbelievable, this whole situation I am in right now.

“Kind of. I mean, not kind of. Exactly.” Miss Breezy is speaking in a more serious tone now, and it is clear now that she is nervous, which also makes me nervous. “Some historical events have roles for you to play, even though the results were already determined. You just need to be there, and do what you can, and things will happen as precisely as they already have.”

I stay silent, waiting for her to continue, because I really do not know what to say for myself. I will just go along with this before thinking too much. I am honestly too confused to do anything other than go along.

She continues: “Unsurprisingly, some events pose a huge amount of danger, which is why you must learn Magic and other lanes of knowledge. All kinds of Magic; all kinds of facts and details. To protect yourself and to come back to…your period of time, which is this period. The books here would be your main source of learning, but I’m also here as your guardian, though I cannot follow you through your leaps through time.”

Protect myself? I try to keep myself composed. “But the results are set in stone.” And I try to understand, repeating what I heard.

“Yes. On that table, clearly placed in the middle, is a diary of missions, a journal of sorts, that contains each event you need to influence.” Her voice is still jittery, a stark contrast from her characteristic heavenliness.

I walk to the table, and indeed, there is a thin leather-bound notebook, which actually looks new somehow. I open it, and the first page reads: ‘6th The Ulsa, 331, Second Era’, and under it reads: ‘A quick fun meeting’. Then an arrow points from the last letter to a second date: ‘19th The Eiine, 477, Second Era’, and under that reads: ‘Travel from Askenny to Isehr. Must cross one mountain. Preparations needed, but not dangerous at all. One week’. A red oval is drawn beside that entry, and in it is: ‘Use carriages, there will be many. No cars. Haha’.

Wow, this looks serious! ‘Askenny’…That sounds like Ashcurn, which is a city I know of. I think our car passed through it on our way to Grandmama’s. ‘Isehr’ rings a bell too.

I flip through the pages, and the entries do not end till every page but three runs out. I estimate around two-hundred entries. That is a lot of periods to jump to. Back to the first page, I notice another date at the very top: ‘Go at The Year 2004 Anam The 23rd The Valvor’.

‘Go at-’…this year? This is the date to jump into this mission. That is in a month! It is so soon! My mind is bursting with thoughts as I try to keep up.

I deliberate over what I have already been told. “Based on…Hm…With so many entries, it’s…clear that I can never actually fail. Right? Because …they’re set in stone…I’m meant to keep going and finish them all, so I’ll always end up safe? But wait one more-” I realise something else, “is the last entry my last one ever?” I inquire, keeping my eyes on the word ‘Haha’.

“No, further entries are simply not recorded here. I know for sure that you’ll always be safe at the end of each…adventure.” Miss Breezy states confidently, with a tone that sounds like she is hinting at something.

“‘For sure’? You’re that certain? Then there must be a solid reason behind that certainty.” I ponder for a short moment before my eyes widen, and I realise immediately, hurriedly flipping to the last page, but it is not what I am seeking, though the theory in my head must definitely be true! It only makes sense, however unbelievable it will still sound.

I decide to voice my thoughts aloud. “I was the one that conjured you. I mean, I will be the one to conjure you, but by going back to the past…in the future.” My head swings at that last line, but I take her silence as encouragement to theorise further. “That’s why I’ve never noticed the blue flash, because it was only placed there recently. The entrance to the Magical woods was just made too. By me? By my future self.” Wow, it really does add up. “I’m feeling very clever now, please don’t tell me I’m wrong.” I huff laughingly and nervously.

“You’re not wrong,” Miss Breezy responds without hesitation. “And I don’t think an entry about that is in the journal.” She deduces the reason I flipped to the last page so earnestly just now.

“All this…” I trail off, gazing at the ceiling.

“Yes,” she confirms.

I will be a very powerful Magician to have created this ‘plane of reality’, and such a marvellous one at that. I feel like I am in a dream, a dream with a plot that is too well-structured, a dream that makes too much sense.

I shake my head from side to side languidly, incredulous. “This is a lot of information to take in in five minutes.” I did wish for an interesting life here in Grandmama’s manor, but this is a whole other level of ‘interesting’.

“From the start, you’re astoundingly good at deductions, a skill that will aid you in many a quest.”

“I’m only twelve,” I repeat the statement I made just now.

“A perfect age to kick off your saga.”

I laugh then, at the total bizarreness of this whole reveal. “So I must make ‘necessary preparations’ before the twenty-third of next month. That includes Magic lessons and research into the background of that period. Where’s Askenny?” Again, I should have brought my phone out with me.

“You have tutors for History and Geography, I’m sure,” Miss Breezy points out, “Focus on the fifth century of The Second Era for now, which I believe consists of many important events around where modern Soria is. At the same time, I will tutor you on Magic, and we’ll start the day after tomorrow.”

I might not have time for picnics once my classes start, though I am told that I can make arrangements flexibly. Maybe I can make something work.

What ridiculous composure I possess! I look down at my palms. Maybe the disbelief will seep in later, after I settle down or when I am lying in bed, but for now I am excited for the many things that are coming. Too excited.

“I see that you’re exhilarated. Very surprising. I had expected you to be more…shocked.” I sense an apologetic smile. “Forgive me for throwing all this onto you so quickly, I’m trying my best.”

“You’re doing an amazing job, Miss Breezy, don’t worry. I…I got this.” Then I laugh again. “And don’t get me wrong, I am shocked, but…also excited. Anxious. Impressed, even.” I turn. “So the ‘coins’ you flipped, the ‘sides’ they are meant to ‘land’ on are already predetermined, and my arrival at the clearing has already been arranged. Is it because of the first Aesena Varinas?” I continue to theorise.

She lets out a small chuckle. “What do you mean, child? You’re the one and only.”

How could that be? “But what about the future me who set this stage? And…” I feel my mind filling up again. “there is another ‘future me’ for my future me, and so on. Aren’t there a million timelines then, supporting its own…‘past’?"

A lot of movies I have watched before spring into my mind, crashing into my thoughts like a waterfall.

“I guess that is an easy way to look at it, but it’s not the case.” I can sense her shaking her head. Well…her ‘head’. “That ‘future you’ you mentioned is just a gear to set this whole wheel into action, as you said: ‘set a stage’. The only Aesena Varinas is the only Aesena Varinas, and soon you will find the best way to explain that to yourself.”

I do not get it. Not yet, at least. But one huge question is reeling me off my patience: “That means…One day, I will be setting the stage for my past-self…” I look around, “but how will I carry so many books?” I twirl, then-oh, I got it! “Don’t answer that, sorry. Magic, of course.” What else? But…“What if I forgot one of the books though, then the timeline…”

“Will not change,” Miss Breezy cuts in. “Somehow, you won’t forget any one of these books. To be concise…,” she hesitates, “your life is already set, the things you will do already arranged. No matter what you do, things will turn out the way they will, and there’s no avoiding nor changing them. You will go through all that is written in that diary without a single difference, no matter what.”

No matter what?

Her tone turns reserved once more. “I understand how that might make your life seem like a boring chore, since your future is fixed and unchangeable and you live just to reach it, but-”

“But the details are not certain.” I finish for her. “I mean, I do not yet know how to achieve that destination, even though I know I will eventually be there.” That also means my life has an…unbreakable insurance…and nothing can truly harm me permanently. “It’s the adventure that is most important, I think, the destination is just a guideline.” I read about something like that before. It was a very good book, I think, though I cannot recall its title…

She becomes silent, and the room does too. “You haven’t changed at all,” she says after a while.

I giggle, understanding immediately. “I guess that makes sense, in a way.” I find myself yawning. Why does information tire me so? I thought my energy would be amped for such an adventure this is becoming.

Miss Breezy notices. “I suppose you must first learn to exit this library. It’s the same as when you came here.”

I hold my hand out, picturing the clearing, and the walls of books blink to become the tall trees, and the lights on the ceiling become sunlight shining down through the canopy.

“That was fun!” I skip about. “Anyway, I have a month, as that journal said.” I should have brought it out with me. Oh well. “No matter what I do, I will be prepared, right?”

“Very right. So you can laze about and throw away precious hours of learning as an experiment of whether or not you will actually turn out to be prepared,” she suggests, “Yet I don’t think your excitement will allow that.”

Hm…“I will test it out some other time, for sure, once I get the gist of how it works. But to be honest, as you said, I can’t wait to start learning.” I yawn for the second time, and then immediately a third.

“The day after.” She laughs, an angelic sound. “Figure out how best to use your time till then. Come, child.” And I follow, skipping all the way as an attempt to push down my mental exhaustion, but I know I am no doubt failing. A nap definitely awaits.
