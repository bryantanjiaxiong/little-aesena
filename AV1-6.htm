1 - 6

I breathe in and out. The green and the blue and the grey and the brown and the yellow and the orange whirling into a single centre…and done! Back in the knowledge-filled wonderland!

“You’re a natural!” Miss Breezy remarks, her voice sounding significantly bouncier with the slight echo here.

I look at my palms. “Was that Spatial Magic? Spatial Ability?”

"No, child, that’s not a thing. You have too many fictional tales in your head."

And I giggle. “But why not? It’s such a cool name for it.”

“Well, there’s already an actual term. It’s partially Conjuration Magic, and also Illusion. A mix of both. And it is Magic, not an Ability, and we will discuss the differences between the two later.” I feel her presence speeding over to the desk, the floating lights glowing brighter as if they are sensors.

Miss Breezy continues: “For now, we will go through the list of what you need to learn-”

I could not keep it in: “The library and the forest are conjurations, and hiding them is the illusion of the log-and-rock? But I sat on the rock.”

She does not miss a beat, having probably expected the question. “Illusions can be physical too. Understand them as distractions. They lure your mind away from the main purpose, therefore no one would think to dispel it there. Though…actually…the log-and-rock is a little obvious. I mean-”

“So a little girl can be curious enough to examine it.” I giggle again. “But it’s also like going to another dimension, isn’t it? Didn’t you say this is a different plane of reality?” I hop around, playing with the swirls and whirls that are so dancing on the floor.

“Ah, I did. Sorry, phrase misused. But I see where you’re coming from. Still, no one would understand if you used ‘Spatial Magic’ to describe anything. Come over,” her voice beckons.

I walk in quick strides. “Oh, that means I’ll meet other Magicians, right? Magicians…Abilitiers?”

“Both. And…Mages, not Magicians. You will need to learn to call things by the right names so people can understand you.”

I nod thoughtfully. “Yet every period of time calls things differently, I thought.”

“Bright mind, Aesena Varinas,” she agrees, “Yes, there’s that too. You have your work cut out for you. Maybe in the future, Spatial Magic might properly be a thing.”

“I will be the one to generalise it!” I lean over the desk. “Anyway, where should I start?” There are papers stacked in very intimidating heaps, and impressively rolled-up parchments and scrolls. And of course, the journal. At a side, drawings of plants and one of a bottle capture my attention, the latter with skilfully drawn bubbles and swirling liquid inside it.

Then they move. Unprepared, I jump backwards.

“What is it?” Miss Breezy notes my sudden movement.

“That’s so cool!” I gather up the drawings, looking at them in turn. Whenever I adjust the angle of looking at one of them, it itself adjusts to my eyes accordingly.

She hums. “That will be one of your first lessons. Documenting, reviewing, organizing, skills that make your duties more convenient so you can better focus on the mission itself.”

I look up towards her voice. “What does that mean?”

“You will learn Magic that can record information for you almost instantly, Magic that can bring to you a specific object like a book from those shelves, Magic that can let you read or take in written information faster, Magic that automatically translates what you say to another language, and so on. Stuff like that. Little things that you might have wished for along the tediousness of life.”

I marvel at the drawings. “I’ll be able to do this by next week?”

Miss Breezy laughs. “Maybe earlier. If you want to learn advanced knowledge or skills, you must first be convenient. Ergo.”

I squint. Hold on, what does ‘ergo’ mean again...? ‘That’s why’, I think. I know I have seen that word before. Whatever. Why am I even thinking about that? This is awesome!

“Let’s begin! Let’s begin!” I am almost hopping.

“Rein in your rushes, youngling.” She has an amused tone. “I know it’s exciting, but for today we’ll mainly discuss on the overall list of things you need to learn, which is the - quote and unquote - ‘syllabus’.”

Aw.

“It won’t be boring, I assure you," she adds upon noticing my expression, “Trust me when I say that there are more things to jump up and down for.”

A strand of hair falls onto my face, and I blow it back up. “Okay then. What other Magic must I learn before the jump?” I put down the drawings.

Miss Breezy hums again. “I’d say defensive ones, but I think Sharp and Screen can cover for that. You know, just in case, you need to have combat skills. You’ll have to utilise your inherited Abilities despite your age,” she pauses for a long moment, and when I am about to ask her about it, she notes: “You have no prior experience, I assume.”

I shake my head. “None. I’d need a license if I want to practice Sharp Magic. And my guardians proba-”

“Let me stop you there. Abilities, not Magic,” she places a heavy emphasis on the word. “Time to have that discussion I talked about.”

“They’re not the same?” I had always thought they are.

“Magic, as you know, is the direct opposite of Logic. Magic makes zero sense.” She waits for me to nod before continuing: “Abilities, on the other hand, can be passed down from parent to offspring, and is therefore dependent on racial biology, which is science, which is Logic, because it makes sense. Understand?”

I lean my head back, wrapping it around what I just heard, then I heave it back forward in a strong nod.

“That’s the shortest explanation I can give you. But, a little trivia: the word ‘Ability’ is derived on one branch from the Latin word ‘habilis’, which means ‘able’, and another branch from the Yehoans, who called the people of Wholeone ‘Abeliyati’, which roughly translates to ‘blessed’.” Wow, Miss Breezy is very knowledgeable on a lot of things all at once.

“Abe-Abeli…How do you spell that?”

“A-B-E-L-I-A-T-I, by the Anglish alphabet.”

Ah, ‘Abeliati’. Close enough. ‘Blessed’, huh? The Yehoans were Men from the continent of Ictalcy that must have found their Men-counterparts in Wholeone extremely bizarre, with Abilities that the Yehoans themselves were not ‘blessed’ with. Yet, that did not stop Yehova from conquering half the continent within two centuries of their arrival.

I actually love Yehoan history. There is much glory and philosophy within its occasional dramatic gruesomeness. With how dominant Yehova was throughout The First Era, there should surely be a few jumps there. A billion people throughout time must have dreamt about visiting the ancient empire, and here I am, a little child entrusted with time-travelling.

I put my hands up, digesting the information. “Alright. So not Sharp Magic, but Sharp Abilities. Same with Screen. And the others? ‘Fire Magic’ obviously sounds much cooler than ‘Fire Abilities’.”

“I believe modern media likes painting the terms in ancient languages. For example…in Graecis, like ‘Pyroabilities’. Is that cool enough for you?” She shifts into a lighter tone.

I burst out laughing. Even I must admit that those ‘spiced-up’ terms are a little awkward. Yet Graecis-Latin and Leomasaen-Latin are often the victims of such ridiculousness.

Recomposing myself, I continue the topic at hand. “So I can never manipulate Fire or Water, or anything at all other than my inherited Abilities, right?”

“That’s not entirely true. While you can’t utilise Fire as flexibly as Fire Abilitiers, you can still use Nature Magic, or - quote and unquote – ‘Elemental Magic’,” she suggests with a huff, “Whatever term your generation likes to use.”

I return into my fits of laughter. Miss Breezy’s satirical tone is unbearable!

She joins in with her chuckles as well. “I mean, you can refer to anything anyhowever you wish, but you need to make sure the listener understands what you’re saying. Absolutely no one in the fifth century will know what is ‘Elemental Magic’, or ‘Pyroabilities’ or ‘Aquabilities’, the last one which is actually quite confused as to whether or not it needs another ‘A’ in the middle.”

I pause to contemplate her last line, then upon comprehension, continue giggling.

“If I ever need to make an article to attack modern pop-culture, I’ll come to you for inspiration,” I quip after I finish.

“Moving on: how goes your understanding on the time-travelling algorithm?” And she returns to solemnness.

“I thought long and hard about it, but I think I’m still missing a few gears. I mean, I don’t exactly know what I don’t understand, but I know there’s something.”

She ‘breezes’ over to the other end of the desk. “That’s a broad area to cover,” she says.

I cannot help but smile at her witty remark. “I conclude that I just need to jump to find out. Actions do explain louder than words.” I feel proud of coming up with that slight word change.

“I guess so. But you need to prepare not only by the means of knowledge, there are also the matters concerning equipment and clothing and routine. You are aware that your jump won’t only last a day, right?” Miss Breezy asks cautiously.

“A week, the journal says.” I sigh, “That’s longer-than-reasonable for a first-time time-traveller. But how much time would have passed in the…original ti-no….present?” I think up another structure for the question. “What will happen here after my jump?”

She muses on the question, and after a short while replies: “That’s random, I’m afraid. But for this first one, I’m sure you’ll not take longer than five minutes from my perspective.”

I widen my eyes. “From your-oh yeah…You’re not coming with me.” I push down the terror that comes with that reminder.

“I cannot. Believe me, I would feel more assured if I could. This fixed timeline of yours that starts you off on such an adventure at such a young age, it makes me feel guilty that there is simply no way around it.” A slightly downcast tone washes over her voice.

“We both know that I’ll be fine at the end of it.” I point out reassuringly, and at the same time reassuring myself.

“Yet what will come before the end, though? I don’t mean to discourage you further, but my duty is to ensure that you’re ready.” She sounds as if she is bracing herself. “It’s fifteen centuries into the past, when there are no phones or electric or the bathrooms that you’re used to. You can conjure a mattress, yes, but…,” she lets out a sharp breath, “sleep might still come difficult. Food might not always be conveniently available, though the journal hints that you will be travelling with other people in a large group, which also presents another obstacle: socializing, and you need to be very swift on that.

“As the journal also stated, you will be navigating mountains, and you might even need to get on a ship if you’re going all the way to Isehr, across…war-torn seas. Even as a thought it is daunting. At best, it will just be exhausting. I worry if it might be more than that.”

The distance between Askenny and Isehr by any route is lengthy and harrowing, and by no means could it be covered in two weeks or even three - or even four, actually - so why does the journal say that it will only take one? And everything that Miss Breezy has pointed out…I-

“You’ve been quiet for a while, are you okay?” Miss Breezy’s concerned tone worries me.

I jump out of my thoughts, then look upwards to the ceiling where the bulbs of light hover and swim by and along the carvings. “I’ve actually pondered on all that you’ve said. Trying to sleep under excitement leads to endless waves of thoughts,” I recall rolling around my bed last night.

I take in a big breath. “With the help of the Magic I’ll soon learn, I think the biggest obstacle will just be the culture shock. And…period shock, I guess. Honestly. I mean, I went camping a few times before, not alone of course…and definitely not for longer than two nights. But still…without a running shower. But surely the migrants will be moving close to water sources, won’t they?” Another thought flashes past my mind. If there are hostile pursuers, however, the migrants would keep a distance from water, since the hunters would definitely think to hunt there.

‘Will’…‘Would’…even though they already happened long ago. Yet they are still ‘will’s and ‘would’s to me.

“As long as I keep going, I’ll be very fine. I’m a little scared, definitely, but I must also admit I’m even more excited than I was yesterday, and I’ll be even more so tomorrow.” I show a thumbs up.

“Hang tight to that personality of yours, Aesena Varinas, it will doubtlessly ward off all that is unpleasant,” Miss Breezy says.

I smile. “What else-wait, um,” before I forget the question again, “do you have any idea as to why this will only take a week? I mean, Isehr from Askenny is pretty far.” They are almost a landmass apart, actually.

“I’m certain it’s your primary objective to figure out, something only you can do, something that the people that will be travelling with you dearly need.”

Hm, I thought so. “Like activating a portal? Or perhaps a rainbow bridge?” I give the second notion a slight comical tone.

“Well, youngling, don’t phrase it as if rainbow bridges are impossible.” Then she chuckles at my reaction. “Just keep an eye out. Something will catch it.”

I nod, shooting a breath. “What else must I prepare? You mentioned clothing…I will learn to do Conjurations, right?” I imagine myself doing dress transformations like Lulala And The Maidens Of The Height. I love that show; there are different transformation scenes for every episode.

She catches on. “I’d advise you to sew them yourself. With Magic, yes, but not via conjurations. Better not risk it being unstable. We will get to those things eventually. You will need to look up the garbs of that period, and the general material they use for clothes. Or…you can just wrap yourself in a big cloak and hide everything underneath.”

“The last one sounds cooler.” I let out a short giggle.

“Then this particular matter is easily settled.” She laughs with me. “I think it will be best to also pick up more elaborate survival skills as soon as possible. Recognizing plants and herbs, cooking quick dishes, hunting…Well, we can do without the last one for now. Starcharting will prove useful in navigation. Since you have the time, you should get all these set up.”

‘Starcharting’, I have never heard the word before, but it sounds so elegant. “I’m good with the suns and moons throughout the year, but my knowledge on the stars could use some work,” I admit, “Miss Alice has astronomy lessons planned for me, and Mister Martin could probably tell me a lot of things about plants. I know a little cooking myself, though it might be a harder without gas.” I think back on all that have been discussed. “This is a really intense syllabus, although of course it’ll be fun to learn so many different things.”

“Beats having stagnant days, doesn’t it?”

“‘The future holds uncharted fun,’ a quote by…I don’t actually remember who said that,” I ponder.

“Oh yes,” Miss Breezy suddenly announces, “I was thinking about one small thing I forgot to say for some time now. It kept slipping my mind. You actually have homework besides going on the missions.”

Ah…Ah?

“Now, now, it’s nothing like what you’re used to,” she addresses the look I probably have on my face, “All these books, every paper and every scroll, as you know, are not simply conjurations. I mean, they didn’t pop out of nowhere. And there is one tiny detail that will only surface to attention if you think hard about it.”

Detail?

“Why do you think the books look new? Did the person who put them there not use them as well? Maybe it is Magic that cleaned them of age, yes, but there is one other explanation that will make more sense with the time-travelling algorithm, if you can connect the dots,” she urges.

I can feel my eyes sparkling at my immediate realisation. “They are new because they are new,” I say as I look around, “They’re all replicated, and brought over from the future via…re-conjuration? Spatial Magic…whatever.” I await what Miss Breezy is about to say with absolute dread.

“Ah, you caught on so quickly. By hand or by Magic, everything must be copied. In a way, that will help you revise as well.” She does not sound very sympathetic at all.

“Impossible.” I spin around and around, staring at the shelves that loom so mightily. Hundreds of them! “All these?” The very notion is out of this world!

“It will be a long time before that specific jump, however, at least a decade, maybe two, and the Magic that you will learn throughout that time will allow you to do such mundane chores in a quick snap. So don’t worry,” Miss Breezy consoles, “Still, I’ll recommend a few more notable titles that you should copy by hand.”

By hand! I squint in disbelief.

“Come to the right side. It’s time for you to see the wings of your desk.”

Wow, what?

Though utterly confused, especially after that explanation on my ‘homework’, I heed her. “I don’t understand.” The things I do not understand keeps piling up.

“Imagine the desk extending, and prepare yourself for a surprise,” she says, deciding to show rather than explain.

So I do, and I do not even need to hold my hand out. The moment I picture the table lengthening, it just does so. Small shelves pop out continuously like blooming flowers in a music video, some with drawers that lurch open before closing as if they are trying to spit out the abundant knowledge kept inside them.

I am frozen, though I should not have been able to feel shocked anymore, considering this whole world of fantasy that I was thrown into.

“Amazing,” I mouth the words, wondering if I actually said them.

“The left side of the desk extends to an alchemy section, with potion ingredients and laboratory apparatus. We don’t need those for now. Maybe next week,” she suggests, “But, um, I think it’s the red one at the front. See it? ‘The Logic Of Magic’, the red book with the purple lining.”

I nod, reaching my hand to it, contemplating the two words. “But that’s literally contradictory to that explanation you gave just now. Or is the title just a clever wordplay?” The book probably lists the aspects of Magic that makes the most sense, such as Magical Science.

“Even nonsensical events have a sort of system around it, and forces of the world that seemingly make no sense must at least make a little, else how could anyone identify or describe it, right?” Miss Breezy pauses for me to nod again before continuing: “So, for our first month, this will be the subject you’ll be studying, alongside our other lessons. In time, you will rewrite the whole book word for word.”

I blink rapidly, and she laughs, and I join in. I stare at the book in my hand, pushing a long breath out of my lungs.

“Well, let’s get started.”
