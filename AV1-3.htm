“Can I guess the questions you wish to ask?”

I chew the sausage roll, almost squeezing a bacon out from the other end. I nod, my mouth full.

"'Why must we meet?', that should be in your head right now. I didn’t properly answer you when you asked."

Light is creeping down the trees on the illuminated side, but I still cannot see the suns yet, though I know it is already midday. I probably should have brought a watch.

I swallow. “Yes, please answer that.”

‘Breezy’ makes a funny sound. “I cannot, at least not yet. Other questions first.”

Then why did she even address the question? I squint at the air.

She laughs at my reaction. “Moving on: ‘What am I?’”

“Yes.” I take another bite, this time a small one. “Not a forest spirit, not a wind spirit,” my eyes widen, “are you the pebble?”

“Goodness, child.” She sounds amused. “Does the name ‘Breezy’ not mean anything?”

I totally forgot to regard that in the possibility I just came up with. “By the way, are you serious with that name? ‘Breezy’? You won’t tell me your real name?”

She huffs. “Again, I didn’t say it wasn’t real. But it’s not my real real name. ‘Breezy’ will do, for now.”

“I shall address you as ‘Miss Breezy’ then.” I guess Magical Beings have more than one name. Or it could be a nickname given by another Magical Being. “Well…what are you?” I go back to the question.

“I am a voice. Just a voice.” Miss Breezy reveals, ‘breezing’ around me, though I notice that the blades of grass do not flow along with her ‘movements’.

A voice? I take a bite. A voice…with a presence? That would explain the ‘core’ talk too. Hm…“Do I have it right if I say that you came into this world via Magic?” I ask. What else can it be though?

“Very right. But don’t ask how. Maybe not yet. Long story.”

There are a lot of ‘not yet’s floating about.

I turn to the depths of the forest, further into the dark-greenness, where there is lesser sunshine piercing down through the canopy. “Is it the Magic of Hawkwoods?”

“No. Contrary to what you might believe, Hawkwoods is still far away. It has its own heavy presence that you’ve definitely not felt before.” She pauses, and I feel observed. “I’m relieved that you feel more at ease now. So…next question:” She turns. Well, she ‘turns’. “‘What is that pebble?’”

I nod. “What is that blue light?” I elaborate my bewilderment on the main factor that led me into these woods.

“Nothing really. It’s just a pebble.”

I remain in my thoughts, not quite registering what Breezy just said, but when my mind finally reaches it, my bewilderment is intensified.

“W-what do you mean?” I shake my head, incredulous.

“Exactly what you heard. It is Magiced, but it’s nothing more than that. Just a simple Magic stone that…does nothing but flash, actually.” There is no mocking tone in her voice. Instead, she sounds rather apologetic.

I adjust my sitting position.

“It’s that random? But like…who placed it there? Why did its flashing grow slower the nearer I got to it? Why is it the only pebble in these woods?” I look around to make sure there are no other pebbles in sight. “Why is the log and the rock positioned in the middle of this clearing? It looks purposely designed to be like that.”

Miss Breezy remains quiet as I launch my barrage of questions.

I compose myself, realising that my sausage roll is gone. I try to look for it before deducing that I have actually finished eating it.

“Why?” I do not know why I asked that nor the answer I am seeking. I move to pick the pebble up, and it does feel like a normal one between my fingers. “Why?” I ask again, probably at the stone.

“You still have buttercakes,” she reminds me, and I go over to take them out of the basket, then plop myself back down on the rock.

“Why have I not seen it shine before? This is my third time on a picnic after all.” I found my question.

“Maybe you weren’t as observant as you were today. Maybe you sat on a different side of the tree. Maybe more heavier things were on your mind. Who knows exactly? The truth we know is that you came for the blue light today,” Miss Breezy reasons. “But I have actually been flipping a lot of coins lately.”

I ponder on her last line. “For us to meet, which we must, but for reasons you don’t feel ready to reveal yet.” I put my thoughts into words.

“They will be a lot for you to take in, which you very soon will. For the moment, a good chat shall suffice.”

One by one, I pop the buttercakes into my mouth. And soon the plate is empty again. Then I lean back onto the log, putting my head on it, looking up to the canopy. Suddenly, I feel tired.

‘A lot to take in’, huh? “This is already the most interesting thing that has ever happened to me in my entire life. I wonder why I don’t doubt it as real. It could very well be a mere dream.” I could have dozed off under my tree, and maybe that is why I have not noticed the blue light before, because there is no actual blue light. My composure seems too steady too, considering the situation.

“You would only know when you wake up. But when in a dream, go as far as you can as long as you can.”

Miss Breezy is right. If I wake up from this now, I would have hoped for it to have gone on a little bit longer, just to know what is up.

With the gaps between the leaves, the canopy above allows me to imagine a green window being cracked, light spilling through it. Maybe someone punched a fist through the glass, which would account for the big hole where the clearing lies beneath.

“Why are these woods so strange?” I keep my gaze upwards. I do not know why I feel that it is strange. It is just…unexplainably so. Some factors are a little explainable though: “I heard birds for a while, but then they’re gone. And there is not a single fly or woodbug around. The grass around the trees shouldn’t have looked so well-tended, but they are. Does Grandmama’s household come in here regularly?

“There are no fallen logs anywhere but this one right here, right in the middle of a clearing. Tell me that isn’t the strangest thing ever.”

Miss Breezy is in her silence again, though this time I think she is allowing me more time to think. But the answer is not that difficult to find, of course.

“There’s Magic. Either Magic built this forest or Magic transformed it into such a place, where only the trees grow taller without any intrusion.” I caress my cheek with my hand. “And this log-and-rock formation is placed deliberately, as I suggested. So is the pebble. Somehow. Whatever for, though?”

“Will you come back tomorrow?” Miss Breezy asks suddenly. “It seems you’ve finished your food, and by your eyes I notice that you’re growing sleepy. Your questions might not have been exhausted yet, I am aware, but you are.”

I guess I am. I rub my eyes, sitting up. I have had too much excitement, and it looks like I am at my productivity limit today.

“I wish to show you something, but you’ll need to have a clear and alert mind for that. Nothing dangerous, just interesting. And fascinating. I’ll wait for you right here,” she assures me.

I stand. “Can’t you follow me back to my room? We can talk there.”

“Alas, child, I’m not just in your head. Everyone would hear me if I speak to you in front of company.”

Hm…“I’m supposed to ask Miss Alice out on my next picnic. Maybe she’ll be busy again. Or I can come after I finish up with her.”

“Anything will do.” Miss Breezy sounds bubbly, almost like she is prancing around.

I look down at the pebble in my hand, this plain grey stone. “Can I take this back with me?”

“Of course. I don’t think it’ll play a big role in anything, however bright it flashes. But some normal things can still be cherished,” she advises. I observe that Miss Breezy loves to preach, though I guess that is everyone around me. I am but a child after all.

“It will be cherished. It will be proof that our meeting happened, and it’ll hold the memory of it too, signalling good things to come, am I right?” I smile at the air, in the direction of where I think Miss Breezy is. It will be so weird to her if I am not actually facing her.

I have earned a keepsake today. It is just a pebble, but in many ways I know I am content with how the day turned out. My mouth suddenly opens and releases a yawn, and I hurry to cover it. I blink back my tears and stretch my arms outwards.

“The woods won’t change on my way out, will they?” It is an unnecessary question. “Can you accompany me out? Only till they end.”

“Come then, child.”

I tuck everything into my basket and pick it up, setting out in the direction where I came from. But though I did note the direction the basket was pointing in, I still doubt myself. I am glad I found Miss Breezy here.

Now that I pay more attention to the giant trees and the canopy roof they support and how in each direction except backwards the woods do not seem to end, I realise the absolute beauty the picture they put together conjures. With less care for my safety and quietness then just now, I can appreciate them as they are. But soon the sun greets me, and I am out of the woods.

“It was a pleasure to meet you, Aesena Varinas.”

I turn and curtsy. “I feel the same, Miss Breezy.”

“I will guide you in the next time you’re here, so don’t worry.”

And I nod and wave before walking off, passing my tree and down the hill. Finally nearing the sidegate, I run down the remainder of the hill, the way I like to do, but this time stopping myself before I could crash into the hedges. I do not want to trouble Mister Martin again. Before turning around a corner, I look back to the woods, and I know Miss Breezy is still watching, so I wave one last time.

I skip through the gates, noticing two figures by the bell on the tower, leaning over something one of them is holding. A book, most probably. One of them has Miss Violet’s general posture, so I call: “Miss Violet!”

The figure turns, and her bright blonde hair proves me right. Behind her, now that I see more closely, is Miss Alice, whose hair is a darker shade of gold.

“You’re back, young Aesena!” Miss Alice leans out of the railing, as dangerously as she did this morning. “Leave your basket at the table, I will tend to it.”

“I can wash the plates myself!” I increase my voice.

“No, no, let us do it. I’m coming down now.” And with that, Miss Alice disappears from sight.

“Go take a shower, my lady,” Miss Violet says, and disappears too.

So I enter the house, and as I was told to do, leaves the basket on the dining table before heading up the stairs and into my bathroom. Too tired for a long wash, I decide that a quick rinse will do, but under the splashing of the water, I find that I can just stand like that for hours.

I wonder how Grandmama would react if I tell her about the log and the rock and the flashing pebble. The pebble! I almost jumped before remembering that I just placed it on my desk. I sigh in relief.

Maybe Miss Alice will like to have a look at it, or Mister Martin. Miss Emilia might know a few forest legends too. Actually, everyone would probably have much to tell me about the subject if I show them the pebble’s Magic.

But I do not think I should have entered the woods in the first place, though they did say that the area around the manor is safe. If they hear about me discovering something Magical, they would most likely not allow me to go out alone again. At least, I think that would be Dada’s and Mama’s reaction, since I did wander pretty deep into those woods. That was irresponsible of me.

Yet there is an incredible urge in me to share the story at dinner later. Perhaps everyone just might commend me on such an adventure. Miss Breezy did not warn me against telling anyone about the experience too. Hm, I wonder why?

Since she wants to show something to me tomorrow. I guess I will decide after that. How much of the story should I tell then? Ah, these thoughts can wait after a nap. I close the tap and exit the bathroom, simply putting on a baggy dress and jump onto my bed, forgetting again to experiment if I can be conscious enough at the end of counting myself to sleep.

I wake with the ring of the dinnerbell, and give my room a loud yawn. Upon seeing the pebble despite the falling gloom, I recall my excitement. The day was not a dream at all. Indeed, it is very real. I jump out of my bed and into the corridor, down the stairs and into the kitchen.

“Good evening, everybody.” I spin on arrival.

“Careful, young miss.” Miss Emilia puts on a frown. “Don’t run in such a long dress. You might slip.” Then she smiles, putting her hands onto my shoulders and guiding me to a chair. “Do you remember the dish you said was ‘spectacularly superb’?”

My eyes widen with joy, and I clap thrice, rubbing my hands together, for in front of me is a large plate of Miss Alice’s and Miss Emilia’s white-sauce curry chicken. The smell of grounded milkbuds immediately wakes my senses to the fullest.

“A little surprise to repay the morning you gave us. Sorry for not calling you to join in our cooking though. You were sleeping so soundly; I didn’t wish to disturb you.” Miss Alice lays out bowls of rice around the table.

Aw, but I do so want to learn this dish. “It’s okay. Thank you so much. I can’t wait. Oh, quickly everyone, please sit.” I give Grandmama a hug before sitting down beside her, and Miss Violet pours the sauce onto my rice. I can feel myself beaming. What good food to tell a story over. A story…Oh no, I have not decided on what to tell yet, and they will soon ask.

But the meal starts with Miss Emilia complimenting Mister Martin about the milkbuds he grew, which ends in an applause from the table. Then Miss Violet tells us of how she asked for the best chicken meat from the market this morning, and how she reached the best deal for it, and we laugh and clap for her too. Then Grandmama comments on the perfection of the dish, saying: ‘spiciness and sweetness are mixed to a delight!’, and this time I start the applause for Miss Alice and Miss Emilia.

Finally, my turn comes.

“How was your day, Aesy?” Grandmama asks, and I just know how I am going to start.

“Miss Alice prepared for me a wonderful picnic, with bread and bacon and cheese and buttercakes!” I spread my hands out. “And in the middle of my outing, I saw a bright blue light flashing deep inside the woods. You know, the one behind the left gate.” I point, wondering for a moment if I am pointing in the right direction.

“A blue light? You didn’t enter the woods, did you?” Mister Martin has a worried expression, and I know at once that I should say no more than that for tonight.

“No,” a straightforward lie, “but I did want to do so badly. It kept flashing, like it’s urging me to,” I gesture as I describe.

“Those woods are very mysterious. You did well to not go for the light alone, young Aesena. Maybe I can follow you next time.” Miss Alice offers, bringing to her mouth a spoonful of the curry and then swallowing. “It will be a great adventure, don’t you think? ‘The Mystery Of The Blue Flash’.” She giggles.

“I’m going out for another picnic tomorrow, to see if it’s still there. It might still be flashing even now.” It is not, of course, at least not in the woods. I wonder if the cars on the road can see a blue flash from my room through the window. “Are you free tomorrow?” I ask Miss Alice.

“I will be taking Alice out in the morning,” Miss Emilia raises her hand, “We won’t be back till dinner.”

“Oh? Where to?” Mister Martin stands up to refill his bowl with curry.

Miss Alice puts her arm around Miss Emilia. “Emilia has been pestering me for days about a birdshow coming to town. Thought I could accompany her. Are you fine with that, young Aesena?” She looks to me.

I nod. “Of course. I might still need help preparing a basket though.”

Miss Violet turns to me with a smile, which I feel myself naturally returning. “I’ll do it this time,” she says, and I smile wider.

Grandmama puts a hand on mine. “I would have gone for the light,” she whispers, winking as she goes back to her food.

The table resumes to other subjects as I listen in: a silverling that has laid three eggs, a bush of starclovers that is starting to sparkle, a new musical composition, an update on the passing of the Butterfly Comet, and so on. Through all that, I keep smiling to myself as I repeat Grandmama’s words in my head amidst my enjoyment of the hearty meal.
