1 - 10

The sun is at the height of day. There is only one. It glares intensely, and I have to hide under a tree for shade. I look back down. Everywhere is sand, and sand is everywhere. These trees are on sand, those trees are on sand, and I am on sand. And it is bizarre how the roots are behaving exactly as they would have in proper dirt, keeping the trees steady and upright.

It is hilarious how one only realises that one still has tons of questions after it becomes too late to ask them. I literally jumped without thinking. ‘Jumped’. Jumped. It does not matter. What really matters is…

What ‘fun meeting’? And with who? How do I look for this man…or woman? Or…God? Maybe the Being Of Time? When do I need to conjure the next Timegate to bring me over to the year 477? How long do I stay in the year 331? What do I do here?

It is too late now.

I readjust my cloak, stepping out into the heat again, heading forward to what looks to be a huge excavation site. I look back to the small pack of trees, to the middle of it where the Timegate ‘jumped’ me out. The gate has disappeared, leaving behind the peace of the isolated greenery and a very disoriented me.

If not for the metallic sound of hammers on nails and stones ringing through the air, I would not have known that there is a giant hole in the middle of the desert. It would have looked like a flat plain desert otherwise, and I would be in deep trouble.

I might even believe that if the hammering halts and if I shut my eyes for a long moment, I would open them to find myself in a very flat desert again.

I stop before the large dip in terrain. Excavation…Ancient artifacts and forbidden sites…Maybe this is a gathering of time-travellers. Maybe they are preparing some kind of initiation ritual for me.

But the people here seem ordinary. Their skin is dark and they are each wearing silver-coloured helmets, and well, the only thing they are preparing for is a year-long project, it seems. Pavilion tents are stood up here and there, and brick sheds litter the perimeter. In the middle, there is what looks to be a temple peeking out of the ground. Pillar and ladders ornament it, and on them a few people are examining the tip of the temple, writing on something in their hands. On the other side, diggers are safely continuing their work.

There are children too. I watch as three of them run to two men who are busy carrying a loose pyramid of bricks between them, but the little ones are pestering them for something. Then the children are each handed a single brick, and they join the adults in walking over to a construction site, seemingly content with their workload. A woman with a pot under an arm then comes over, takes away their bricks and shoos them away, and the men laugh.

I cannot help but smile at the scene. Bored kids are difficult to satisfy. I was recently a bored kid too. Before…

What should I do, though? Should I go down? I am guaranteed a fun meeting…but would these people welcome a foreign stranger into their midst? Yet, I am a little girl, so maybe they will. These people look to be of Kaettlein-kind, but despite their complexion, their skins do not have the racial Kaettlein dustiness. Maybe I am just too far away to see clearly. Are they just dark-skinned Men? I will just keep watc-

Hm…Among the people there is one fair-skinned participant: a young woman with her long black hair tied up in a ponytail, a black stream that smoothly trails down to just above her hips. Unlike everyone else, she is not wearing a helmet, uncaring for her own safety. She dons a plain white baggy shirt, and long baggy pants of brown which gets more of a dirt brown at her legs. She definitely stands out.

Standing back up from bending over to look at something, she exchanges words with those same children I saw, then after they run off she uses a sleeve to wipe off the sweat on her forehead.

And our golden eyes meet. Instantly I recognise her. How could I not? A little shorter in height – well, not exactly a little – and she would be the spitting image of my reflection. She smiles, probably recognising me too, then she waves to me, goes over to a table to pick a satchel up before starting to come over.

As she uses her Screen Abilities to make platforms in the air in order to elevate herself up to where I am, I keep my eyes on her, dumbfounded. She hops off the last platform, which fades the moment her feet leave it, and reaches me. She calls a large Screen on top of the both of us, letting it act as a shade against the sun.

“Hello,” she greets, keeping her distance, not wanting to alarm me.

But I already know what is going on, ‘a fun meeting’, so…

“Hello,” I greet her in return.

A silence falls.

Then I pick my voice back up. “You’re beautiful,” I say. She is stunningly gorgeous, and her worker’s outfit does not diminish that fact at all. And she has a strong presence about her, a strong Magical presence. The whole world seems to gravitate towards her.

“And you’re adorable!” She breaks into a wide smile then, and of course I smile in return. She is even more beautiful when she smiles. “Aesena Varinas.” She extends a hand to me.

I huff out a giggle before taking it. “Aesena Varinas,” I introduce myself in turn. Then we erupt into a fit of giggles, still shaking hands.

“How are you feeling?” She puts her hand back on her waist.

“Wouldn’t you know?” I quip.

“It just seems like the question to ask, you know. You’re on your first mission in a faraway time. I would know.” She cannot stop smiling.

I cannot too, of course. Of course. Of course. It is like this joyful sensation cannot be held back, plastering on my face a permanent grin. “I…should have seen this coming.” Of course. This situation should have been so obvious. Miss Breezy must have been aware about this too. Of course!

“I would know,” she admits. And we erupt in another series of giggles.

“How long do I stay here?”

“Only for a short while. Less than hour. Probably twenty minutes.” She looks to the excavation site as the wind blows strands of hair from her face. She is incredibly beautiful.

“Is that so? I thought you would give me some kind of training or…or something like a lesson or something.” Is this literally just a fun meeting then? Well, not ‘just’, but, it could be more.

She turns back to me with her eyes widened. “Disappointing, right? Very! I felt the same back then too. Can’t do anything about it, though.” She spreads her hands out, the satchel swinging. “Oh yeah. There’s more to our little chat, actually. Kind of a delivery from me.” She reaches into it and takes two books out before handing them to me. “The index of the library and a summary of time-travelling.”

“Oh! Wow, cool!” Gold!

“You must be missing this, but it’s for good reasons that you’re only given them now. Reasons that…,” she trails off.

“Reasons that I’ll find out myself,” I finish for her.

“Yup.”

“Did you, um…Have you conjured the library yet? The log-and-rock and the endless forest?” How powerful is she? “In the past?” I add as an elaboration. I did not need to, of course.

“No, not yet. I think I’m still a few years from that. But I’m heading back there too, after this mission. It’ll become a bit more different through the years.”

Something tells me I should not ask what she meant by ‘different’. Details, details. “How’s Miss Breezy?” I ask instead.

“My very best friend. I can’t wait to tell her about how adorable you are!” She smooths her fingers through my hair, and I giggle.

Can I ask about the manor too? But…

“How-how old are you?” I wonder if that question sounds rude, but it could not be so. She would know.

She shows me two fingers, wiggling it. “Twenty-two in a few months.”

Ah, it is the month of Ulsa for her, three months from Oscy. Oh, wait, that is not right; this is not her time. This situation has dizzed me a little. But anyway, twenty-two is a bit too far into the future. “Are you already this beautiful at sixteen?” I feel impatient.

She huffs. “Maybe. But you shouldn’t chase adulthood. At least not with too much of a sprint. If I could, I would have held onto my cuteness a little longer. Though, it’s not every day that I get called ‘beautiful’ by myself.”

I blush. Then…“Are there any dan-” I stop myself just in time. ‘Are there any dangerous missions that I should look out for?’ and maybe even ‘Will I meet any trouble in this first mission?’. Details…Details about the future should be avoided.

She has already been through many time-travelling missions, meaning that she knows how dangerous some of them might be. But I myself do not know that yet, and that is a huge difference. Once she tells me, I would then know, and then those dangers would become unavoidable. It is not like the dangers are avoidable now, but…at least I do not know they are coming my way.

It is like the Coloured Square Effect. The Ball-In-The-Box Effect in the movies.

Also, if I had asked those questions, many memories might surface in her mind, and she would recall those dangers even if she decides not to inform me of them. Then, in a similar way, those dangers would be fixed onto the timeline, unavoidable, inevitable, simply because they exist in her memory, indelible.

She notices, of course. Of course she does. “My turn for a question, if I may?” And I nod, blushing again as I realise the amount of questions I have thrown her way, though I still have so many more. “What are the pros and cons of our duty? Not ‘cons’, per se, but…you know?”

I nod again. “I know.” ‘Things we must avoid’, she means. Something like that…

I structure my answer: “We have a total guarantee that our life has purpose, and that the destination we’re heading to is actually somewhere. We know, that in the end, we will find some kind of success, no matter what.” I pause for a breath, one in then one out.

I breathe in again. “But on the other hand, we can never ask how, because we shouldn’t. We must wait for the ‘how’s to come to us themselves. We must wait for our life to find its own way, just like everyone else, though they don’t have our impulsive urges.” Urges which I will have, urges which I must supress. “We cannot ask for a guidebook, like…like fortune tellers, because then our fixed timelines will be…even more fixed?” It does make sense, though.

She gives me a look of approval, then her golden eyes turn solemn, though she is still wearing a smile. “Yes, never submit to a seer’s demands. Their Magic to see into the future is a direct counter to ours.”

I want to ask if she has ever experienced having her future read by a seer, but I know better. I think I understand most of the time-travelling algorithm now. The journal is without much details for the same reason. Did I not say a similar thing that day? That day I learnt of my duty.

“Ignorance is bliss in this particular case,” she continues, “Ours.”

“Yes,” I agree.

“I can’t believe I was so cute and intelligent,” she remarks, “Only twelve and already as wise as a thousand.” She is exaggerating, and I laugh at that.

“You said the same thing yourself, surely,” I suggest.

“Word for word, I think, but I don’t quite remember. You’ll be impressed the same way I am now when it’s your turn to be standing opposite the girl who is on her first mission.”

“No matter what,” I state.

“No matter what,” she concurs.

I put the two books she gave me into my Magic Bag, then I face back to her. “So…” I start to say.

She hums. “One more thing.” She approaches, taking something out of her pocket. A necklace. She reaches around my neck and puts it on for me.

I stare at the pendant, radiant and reflective: an overlapping ‘A’ and a ‘V’ on a series of circles, and the whole symbol is inside a main circle like some kind of set diagram. Then I remember reading about it: old clocks that use circles instead of sticks as their hands.

“Your insignia,” she explains.

Woah. “A trademark…Like…copyright?”

She laughs. “Yes. Like copyright.” She reaches into her collar and shows me her own pendant. “See? The very same.”

“Thank you,” I am filling up with bubbling confidence, “I must make another one in preparation of your today, then?” Words get a little tongue-twisty whenever the topic of time-travelling is being discussed.

“Not very soon, but you’ll find out,” she assures.

I tuck the pendant into my cloak. “Is it about time…now?” I wonder if I will ever get to meet my future-self again. And again, I cannot ask. But maybe…No, never mind.

“I think so. My mission here in this period is this meeting with you, and helping them solve the mystery of this ancient monument. The Shaping brought a lot of strange stuff into the world.” She gestures to the excavation site.

I tilt my head. “The…Shaping?” I have never heard that term before.

“Oh, The Shifted Heavens Phase. ‘S-H-P’, therefore ‘Shaping’, see? It’s a slang that became widespread on the media. Also, you know, the re-shaping of reality and all,” she explains, then our golden eyes meet again, “Your timeline is not there yet.” She laughs, and I ponder on the information.

A moment after, her expression changes. “Anyway…are you scared?”

I smile. “Definitely.” I would be incompetent if I am not.

Her eyes gleam. “Have faith in your personality, and you’ll always turn out more than fine.” She places a hand on my shoulder, then she backs away, and the Screen above us dematerialises. “I’ll take a step back now, and-”

“Do you still use ‘Yippa’?” It is a random question, but I just want to know.

She briefly looks at me with a confused expression, her mouth opening a little and her head slightly tilted, before she bursts out in laughter. “Oh my, wow, I really forgot about that.”

‘Forgot’? What doe-Then I get it. Oh…

‘No matter what’, the phrase rings true no matter what. The question escaped her memory of my today, but I still want to try it myself though I know I will also fail. I will remember to note this down later. Keyword: ‘Yippa’.

She produces a bracelet from her left sleeve, a mere string of dark blue, and hanging from it is a seemingly very normal grey pebble, but I recognise it, my fingers feeling my own pebble in my own pocket. “My lucky charm.” She unlatches the pebble from the bracelet.

“And here you will learn from me the Throwing Spell.” She grins at my eyebrows being raised. “You haven’t heard of it, no doubt; I made it up for my leisure hours. You can actually do almost anything with your mags. Here, watch.” She lifts the pebble to her lips, detachedly whispers: “Hohoho,” then she swings her fist backwards, gathering momentum.

I start to protest, but the pebble is shot off into the sky with the hurling speed of a catapult. Then in the distance, it shines, and the farther it goes the faster it shimmers, like a blinking star on the endlessness of this desert that is so laid out like a blank canvas. I am struck speechless.

“Yippa,” she calls. And in a split three seconds, the shine slows and disappears and then she snatches the pebble from the air. “Does that answer your question? You can keep doing it, repeatedly. A rocking way to pass the time, right?” She looks at me expectantly.

I look down at my own pebble.

She becomes alert. “No, no, no. Don’t do it yet. Not here and not now,” she giggles, closing her hands over mine. “Honest opinion here, but your skills are still immature. Hone them and then try it yourself, okay? I usually do this on cliffs where I can just sit and see it blink away like shooting star. Okay?”

“Okay,” I cannot keep the smile off my face.

“Here, give me your hand and the pebble.” And when I do, she makes a motion with her fingers, and a blue string wraps itself around my wrist, on it a silver latch. She clips the pebble onto it.

“I know what you’re thinking,” she steps back, “that you’ll not forget to do such a thing to your younger-self when you meet her a decade later. But you will. You will forget just like I did.”

“No matter what?” Of course.

She smiles back, then she gestures at the desert. “You can still try, because I did.” We share a little laugh.

It is time. And without thinking, I wave a hand, and from nowhere springs the mirror-like Timegate. It is as if the graphics of this reality suddenly glitches before a hidden feature manifests itself.

“It’s very nice meeting you, Aesena Varinas,” she bids me farewell, and her eyes are filled with a longing I do not understand.

“I feel the same, Aesena Varinas,” I respond in kind. It does not feel strange at all, which is a strange fact ironically.

We wave at each other, and then I step through the gate. And I wonder if I am repeating the same mistake of not asking all of my questions. But the Timegate swallows me.

What greets me on the other side is a lush green jungle. Dry mud cakes the ground, with bushes of fern and wildflowers sprouting out like toppings. Trees loom mightily, though not as mightily as the trees in the forest back at home; these trees are more densely crowded, putting me in a sinister atmosphere, as if I am being pressed down by the heavy greens.

Through an opening in the leaves above my head, I marvel at the distant heights of The Essarian Mountains, their peaks crowned by clouds and mists. The peak of Mount Terra looks the very same even though I am fifteen centuries into the past, a huge spike that protrudes into the sky, one much higher than its peers.

I hear a river, and when I listen more closely, I hear the neighing of horses and the sound of wooden wheels grinding the ground. I turn around, seeing pillars of black smoke reaching into the sky, smoke from a dozen campfires.

I pull my cloak tighter around me, then check if my Magic Bag is by my side, then check if the bracelet with the pebble is around my wrist. I breathe in. Then I breathe out. Then I breathe in. I smile.

So it begins!
