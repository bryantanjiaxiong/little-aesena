1 - 2

The leaves above sing along with the wind, and for the third time, one lands onto my head with a light pat. I giggle as I swipe it off, almost pulling out a lock from my twintails. I rearrange my hair.

I like this spot. The ground is a little steep, but that means it is easier to straighten my legs. The small road below is often empty, sometimes for as long as I observe it, and when a car does pass I can look at it trail along the road like a toy-car set and attempt to block it from my vision with a thumb to see if I could predict its speed as I follow its route.

And I also like to imagine that this specific tree enjoys my company. It probably has not had a child have a picnic under it before me. I wonder how the whole area would look like up on its highest point. To the left is Grandmama’s manor, to the front the road and the Growmany, to the right…a lot of densely packed trees, and to the back…even more trees.

It would be marvellous to behold them in a complete panoramic spot. Maybe someday I will climb up, when someone else is around. Miss Alice is a great climber, she told me. I saw her repair the roofs before, going up and down without a ladder, scaling the storeys with ledges and window frames and jutted ornaments.

Perhaps the days here will be filled with interest once the lessons start. That is why I am so eager for them. And perhaps I should have brought a book with me before coming out, maybe one on History or…Actually, a novel would do. There is food enough for three servings, since I noticed a dozen slices of buttercake under the packaging of the toasts. Miss Alice must have put them in there when I was not paying attention. I will be here for some time.

Well, the scenery entertains, at least. I turn to where the basket is for another slice of bacon. I am curious as to how it might taste between two toasted bread with the cheese omelette. And after trying this combination, I will add a sausage in the next one, making it a roll. Oh, I almost forgot the clouds. They must join in the fun as well, these fluffy chewy lovelies.

A blue flash catches my attention, and I face it. But there is nothing. I swear I saw-Ah, there it is! What is it? And it disappears again. Is it twinkling?

Deep within the woods behind the tree I took as my favourite, a single blue light beams and vanishes. In four-second intervals, I count. It is not really bright, for it does not cast the trees in blue glare on its way to my eyes, but it is very distracting: A bead of blue, sparkling alone among the shadows of the oh-so-tall trees, where under the trees is just flat grass, not even a bush of fern or flowers in sight.

It is a dark green floor with hundreds of tall brown pillars supporting a roof of translucent lime green, having a bit of yellow spilling here and there but otherwise is dim and gloomy. And the blue light lies ahead, far within, but it continues to flash as if it is so near, giving me the urge to just reach out and close my hands around it, as if the woods make the pit of a schoolbag, and I am fumbling in search of…the blue light.

I block it with my thumb, to see if my thumb could be outlined in blue when the light flashes. And it is. I laugh then, and turn away, deciding not to give it too much thought, though my mind easily wanders back to it.

What could it be? A fallen star? Ridiculous. How was that the first thought that came…Oh, a torchlight? Though who could have ventured into these woods without stumbling on the manor, which one could have clearly seen even from where the blue light is? Hm, could the manor be seen?

In the corner of my eye, I can still see it flashing. Or I am just imagining it, then the blue light is truly calling out to me. I turn to it again with a somewhat excited sigh of hesitation.

‘Look for something different in every day,’ Grandmama’s words echo.

But there is no one here to take care of me. And I did not bring my phone out, though it would not do me any good in a forest even if I did. If I remember correctly, these woods would eventually lead to Hawkwoods, a place that inspired many a horror story.

The many storybooks and children’s tales I have read have one constant reminder, that things like approaching a blue light shining deep inside a shadowy forest can only mean trouble: a trap laid by goblins or kidnappers, a portal to another realm, a collective glare of a dragon’s treasure trove that…suddenly accumulated, a cursed artifact dropped accidentally by an evil sorcerer, a fallen star that might explode any second now…

There is no stopping my curiosity and impatience, however. I pack up my stuff just in case I might get lost, so at least I have something to eat for a while. I cannot believe that that is the one solution my mind went to. The blue light could be something extremely dangerous for all I know. Anything near Hawkwoods is potentially filled with unpredictable Magic.

But it could also be something I can only look for once in my life, and I will definitely not miss it.

The day has been going well so far, having started with preparing those cups of tea. Surely it will end nicely, right? I really hope I get to have dinner back in the manor tonight, yet these worries cannot stop me.

‘The legs of the young are meant for adventures,” Grandmama likes to say.

Oh well, here goes nothing. I stand up, smooth my skirt down, and head in the direction of the woods, and slowly the singing of my tree’s leaves grow faint, replaced by the eerie roars of the woods. Here the leaves sound fiercer, as if they are warning me to stay out.

Yet I go on further. To my relief, my ears pick up the chirping of birds. I look up to realise that the leaves and branches make a lovely canopy, and suddenly the woods do not feel as scary as before. I feel relaxed again, but the continued flashing of the blue light reminds me of the still-present danger.

I look backwards, spotting my tree, deciding that it is time to start memorising trees as landmarks. Despite their imposing sizes, they are loosely positioned. I will also be walking straight anyways, so the danger is lessened, though Magic could easily misdirect me and set me on an aimless wander. If that is so, it would already be too late now.

I make sure to tread softly, just in case there is truly a kidnapper or a goblin or a troll or an evil sorcerer in wait. I observe: A tree that has three juts on its trunk at the height of my waist, a tree with its trunk covered with moss up to the height of my knee, a tree that makes an almost-perfect circle with its leaves in the canopy above, a tree with a trunk of an obviously lighter shade of brown, a tree with a lot of fallen twigs around its bottom.

It is probably one of the worst tracking tactics that anyone has ever thought of, but I have faith in my brain storage to keep all this mental images.

Is the light flashing slower? I pause, tripping a little, almost losing my attention on the light, which if I had would have jeopardised my entire situation. I keep my eyes forward, in the direction of the blue light that is no longer there, and I wait. Sure enough, it appears again, but in a much slower fashion.

And it fades. I start counting. Is the battery of the torchlight dying? Is the portal closing? Is the artifact losing its power? Is the star going to explode after all? Is the trap sensing-

Eight seconds!

I breathe in, and I breathe out. And I breathe in again, and I breathe out again. Should I go further? I am almost there, but should I? Perhaps this is my last chance to do so, and maybe I would forget all about this if I turn and run back to the manor. Or I would beat myself up for chickening out, then never knowing what the blue light could be.

I keep walking, but slower now. Very slow, with feathery steps. I notice another tree that could be a landmark, this time with a huge root pushed out from beneath the ground. I remember: juts, moss, circle, brown, twigs, root. They are only to assure myself that I am going the right way on my way back later.

Nine-second intervals now, I pause to count, or near nine seconds. The leaves roar again, and I can no longer hear the birds. There is not a single insect too, which is peculiar. I have an urge to look upwards but I know if I do I might have a hard time looking for the right direction again. Even now I doubt if I am going the right way when the blue light fades, with every direction looking the same without it. I should probably only walk when it shines.

Look forward…Look forward…

Ten seconds and then eleven seconds, closer and closer. I deduce that the light flashes slower the closer I go. Either that or it is dimming and I am running out of time to get to it.

The portal is closing…Where does it go? Why does it-

Fifteen seconds. I am using too much time stopping to count and too much time stopping to doubt my direction. I hasten my pace, though still keeping my footsteps soft. I start to take bigger steps, like when I was tiptoeing to the toilet or to the kitchen in the middle of the night.

It flashes again, and when it fades I realise that it is just a few steps away now, somewhere in front of a large log that is on an almost-flat rock, the first log on the first rock I have seen since entering these woods. I almost cannot believe that I have finally reached it. It flashes once more, and I continue walking towards it.

It is clearly not a fallen star or a treasure trove or a portal, though it could still be a trap or an artifact or a dozen other things.

I arrive at a small clearing, where in the centre is the log and the rock and the light that has faded. I put down my basket with the handle facing backwards to indicate the direction I came from, an action I almost forgot, with the urge to look up so compelling.

The canopy is broken here, the sunlight pouring in, splashing onto the trees on one side but not reaching the ground, though here it is not as dim as my walk through the woods.

I wait, and I wait, counting, but the flash does not reappear. A minute passes, and the log and the rock remains a log and a rock, and nothing else. Another minute goes and my impatience is pricked.

Then an idea strikes. I step backwards, putting some distance between me and the clearing then putting more. And the blue light flashes again. Then I run at it before it starts fading, though it still fades, but I manage to keep in mind its position on the rock.

I pant, finding myself looking at a pebble. I blink twice, then I blink thrice more, then I squeeze my eyes shut for as long a moment as my excitement can grant me, and when I open my eyes the pebble is still a pebble. And it is not like those shiny reflective pebbles or white smooth pebbles. It is not even blue.

It is grey and not rounded, a little like a one-corner-longer pyramid when I am observing it from one angle and from another it is an oval with slight juts. It also seems meticulously placed, at the centre of the rock’s surface just in front of the log.

I cannot believe my eyes. What-How-Why-How did a pebble like this shine, and with such a strange pattern? Why did it stop flashing when I am near to it? Why does it look so normal? This is rather anti-climactic.

Well, this is a sloppy bait if this is a trap, though since it got me here, one could say it worked quite well. But it is not a trap. This feels too random.

I look around once again. Truly, the woods look the same everywhere. The pillars that are the tree trunks loom like slender giants, and the leaves paint a green night sky with billions of stars with a large sun at its centre. There is only silence hanging in the air, and it seems as if it is waiting. I wonder what it is waiting for.

I start walking the perimeter of the clearing, all the while looking at the pebble sitting there. Perhaps it would burst into flames or transform into shiny goo should I pick it up, so I dare not. Maybe the pebble is not normal after all.

My mind is swamped with tales and folklore that I have read before as I try to remember anything that could give me some insight into this situation. But I got nothing. This is a mystery. Either that or it is a random Magical Happening.

Why are there no other rocks? This is weird. Why is this pebble the only pebble here? Where did it spring from? There are no rivers here, and the sea is far away, and the winds do not feel strong at all so far below the height of the canopy. Did a bird place it here, perhaps? Well, some winds can carry stones, with Magic and all that. And this is near to Hawkwoods. But that cannot be the only answer to this riddle. Magic is too broad a topic. So Magic how?

Oh…Oh! Oh! It is an egg! It could be. It would make sense. Wait…no it would not. What kind of uncaring mother would lay an egg without supplying it heat or nest? Why is it not here protecting it?

I sigh in disappointment. I cannot think of anything. What a letdown! I have spent so much time to get here too. The sunlight looks about near midday. Perhaps I should be relieved that this did not end up being dangerous, but I cannot help feeling deflated, as if the day had betrayed me. Should I at least take the pebble back? It is still unexplained why it can flash.

“Hello, child.”

I jump, almost falling backwards. I steady myself and look around, looking at the basket and getting myself to crouch position, ready to sprint.

“Forgive my startling you. I didn’t know how to begin.”

I look up. The voice sounds to be coming from above. Female, echoey, angelic, resonating, clear, I note, like a woman from an animation movie with a name I cannot recall at the moment.

“Please don’t run. I won’t hurt you.”

That is the worst possible line to assure someone that one is harmless.

The voice is definitely coming from above, though it also feels to be…around me. She feels Magical. A wood spirit? A guardian of that…pebble? A-

“Are you God?” The question slips out of my thoughts. Is God a woman?

She laughs then, a heavenly sound, and the leaves roar with her. “No, child, I’m not Him, nor any of Them.”

‘Them’? What does she mean?

“Who are you?” I ask instead.

I can almost imagine a woman in glowing silk robes bending down to observe me curiously. “You must probably be regarding me as a forest spirit, but I’m not exactly one. I would give you my name if you give me yours, child.”

I hesitate. There is no apparent harm in telling her. “My name is Aesena Varinas.”

She sighs, almost in relief for some reason. “And you can call me Breezy.”

Something tells me that I have just got tricked, though in a trivial way. “I can call you ‘Breezy’? But that’s not your name, is it?”

“You’re very bright, child. Interesting. I seem to have just admitted that you’re right, didn’t I?” She laughs again. “You’re not right, however, but ‘Breezy’ will do.”

I must go around this carefully. “Okay, ‘Breezy’, what do you mean…‘begin’?” I slowly word the question.

She becomes silent, probably trying to understand my meaning, then she remembers. “Ah, I meant that I didn’t know how to greet you.”

I ponder. “‘Greet me’…um…why?” Perhaps it is because I am intruding. “Is this your domain? Are you tied to this clearing?” I turn back to the log and the rock, and when she speaks again, I turn to her. Well, not ‘her’ actually, but I need to imagine I am facing something.

“Oh, no, no. You just called me ‘Breezy’ yourself. I can go anywhere. And to answer your first question: we needed to meet.”

That gives me another guess. “Are you a wind spirit?” I ask quickly after.

She hums, a melodious sound. “Can’t exactly say I am one either. No, I’m not any kind of spirit.”

Then her last line to my question before hits me. ‘We needed to meet’?

I am baffled. “Hold on, what do you mean? Why do we need to meet? And if you can go anywhere…why did you greet me here? Why not when I was outside the woods?” I feel like I am interviewing someone.

“That’s a lot of questions, child.” She pauses, then she hums again. “Honestly? I thought that if you really enter the woods to seek out the blue light that you saw, then I would greet you, otherwise I would leave you be, for maybe it’s not the right time. It’s like flipping a coin.” I can almost see a grin in the air.

Still confusing, but…“It’s heads then,” I remark playfully.

‘Breezy’ seems to have ‘breezed’ to the opposite side of the clearing, and so I turn as well, going along with my imagination. “Not quite. We should not discriminate. It could be tails for the more positive choice. Though, what do you think? Is this the more positive outcome?”

“I’m still finding out.”

And she ‘breezes’ around again. “You’re already very intelligent as a youngling. Too intelligent. And I don’t only mean it as a praise. It is a clear observation.”

I turn and turn, keeping up with my imagination, though is it really my imagination? Maybe I am sensing her presence…or something.

“You’re also able to pinpoint my main location,” she continues, “I mean, where I mostly am. Do you understand? I mean-”

“The core?” I suggest. Then it is not my imagination after all.

“Precisely. Though…” she trails off.

“Not exactly,” I finish for her, and she laughs, louder than when she laughed before. I find myself laughing too.

“A great command of your language too. Very fluent.” Her observing me somehow feels patronising, like a military officer sizing up a recruit. “And very careful in every step you take. You are still ready to run should this go south, aren’t you?”

I contemplate my situation. “It would still be too late, but I can get myself some distance, then luck will continue for me.”

“Ha! Grand answer.”

The leaves above sings, then quietens. “It won’t turn south, will it?” I want one last confirmation.

“No, I assure you, it won’t,” ‘Breezy’ confirms, “So sit, please, continue your picnic under the song of the canopy. We have much to talk about.”

I look again at the log and the rock. “About the pebble? Did you put it there?” I gesture to it.

“Not really about the pebble. And no, it has been there for quite some time, along with the log and the rock, but that’s a very long story. A very remarkable one though, but it’s meant for some other time.” Every word she speaks somehow wakes my senses up, her soothing voice clearing my mind.

“We have much to talk about?” I repeat what she said, tilting my head.

“Don’t we, Aesena Varinas?”

I do still have tons of questions. Why do we need to meet? What is that pebble? Why does it shine blue when it is not blue? Can we have our conversation outside the woods? Could she be a pixie flying about with a microphone?

There is a thrill coursing through my veins, reverberating with my relief to have come for the blue light. Where is it leading me?

I realise that I am smiling. That must be a good sign that I am comfortable with this. And so I take the remainder of the fried food out, careful not to move the basket, and sit onto the rock, ready to listen.
