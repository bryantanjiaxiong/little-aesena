1 - 7

Woah. Just how many conjurations is the illusion of the log-and-rock hiding?

I reel backwards, disoriented.

It is like a ripple, how the forest speeds away from me in every direction, the clearing widening, like standing in a sea of green as the water recedes outward in a circle. Like the legend: The Maads And The Parting Of The Ocean. Like a Water Ring.

As the field replaces the forest, the log-and-rock transforms into a row of weapon-stands. Bows, daggers and swords and even pistols are displayed in shining splendour, each with detailed carvings and ornaments, totally a scene from some kind of ancient treasure armoury.

I regain my footing and recollect my senses. “Every day, a new surprise.”

“What a life, right?” Miss Breezy’s voice is as clear as the sky.

"I assume," I gesture around me, "my training ground." And it is huge! "Are there any more venues the log-and-rock can lead to?" A waterfall and a lake would be really nice now that my imagination is running free. Or a meadow with singing flowers and dancing animals.

“All in good time,” she answers mysteriously.

“Those are real guns,” I point, “Am I going to learn to use all these?” The notion does scare me a little. Weapons are only pretty on a television screen.

“Well, um, all in good time,” she repeats, but this time with a hesitant tone, “Most of these are too heavy for you at the moment. You can manipulate their weight to suit your perception when you learn the right Magic, which leads to our main reason here: Magic and Abilities.”

“Both?” I almost leapt in excitement.

“We have until evening, right? That’s plenty of time, so why not?”

I have another Chemistry class with Miss Violet tonight. With the past few days only focusing on written knowledge whether if it was with Miss Emilia or Miss Violet or if it was here with Miss Breezy, this physical exercise feels refreshing.

Finally, some actual action!

“You have memorised that list of incantations, yes?”

I put my bag down by the stands to take the scroll out. “Yup!” I look through the words again.

“Tell me, Aesena Varinas, the workings of Incantation Magic,” Miss Breezy prompts a revision.

I look up with a ponderous tilt of my head. “Mages have their own Magical Particles that they can control with…um…voice commands. The incantations are like passwords, and a mage must first assign a ‘password’ to a spell before using that very spell, so that the Magical Particles know how to perform.” I squeeze my brain to see if I have missed out anything.

“Oh!” I remember. “A mage must constantly manifest her, or his, own Magical Particles because spells can very quickly use them up.” In a way, these rules make sense. ‘The Logic Of Magic’.

“Not everyone can manipulate Magic, but everyone has their own biologically inherited Abilities, at least in modern times. You are one of the lucky ones.” Her voice is fluffier on that last line. “Let’s start th-ah, yes, and there is actually a short-form for the term ‘Magical Particles’: ‘Mags’.”

“‘Mags’,” I repeat, “Like ‘magazines’, like bullets in pistols and rifles? Bang, bang!” I make a finger-gun.

Miss Breezy laughs. “Very observant, youngling, this is the first time I made that connection. Yes, it’s fitting, isn’t it? Magical Particles are like ‘magazines’ for mages.”

I turn from the weapon stands to face the vastness so cleanly laid out. “So…what do I do? To manifest my mags? And do I just assign ‘passwords’ to them in my head? Or must I say it aloud?”

“First, try to concentrate on the air around you, for there are already mags prepared for you. The log-and-rock illusion and the conjuration of the library, how you get in and out, all those have used up some of the mags, but there are still plenty.”

Of course, now it makes more sense, but…“I didn’t use incantations for any of them.” Oh wait, no. I did hold my hand up whenever I needed to enter or exit the library. “The ‘passwords’ can be actions too, but I didn’t actually assign any…Did my future-self assign it…indirectly? Or is it directly?” My mind swings.

“Don’t you concern yourself over that. It’s kind of a different scenario. As I said, your skills will eventually transcend even the logical boundaries of magic. You’re not expected to understand how all that works,” she explains, before adding: “Or at least, not yet.”

I try to feel the air. There is no wind, yet it is not stuffy, and nor is it hot. It does not smell like anything either. As Miss Breezy said, this huge land is a conjuration inside an illusion. I do not really understand it, but everything here, including the air, is surely made from Magic. That means, as she also said, there are leftover mags, and they should be everywhere.

But I do not know how to sense them, or control them. “And the…assignment of the incantations?” I repeat my question.

“Pick a spell from that list, think hard about the incantation and picture how the spell works in your mind. Focus with emphasis. It might be a bit tricky at first. I’m sure you must feel confused as to how to feel the mags around you, but you will know when you know. Just go for it.”

I browse the list. “I’ll try the ‘Conjure A Stone’ one,” I decide. ‘Limipetram’.

‘Focus with emphasis’.

I shut my eyes and hold my hand out, focusing on the word and trying to manifest the picture of a stone in the back of my mind, how it is hard and solid and rough and brown or grey. I immediately feel something click, and I almost opened my eyes in surprise but I manage to keep them closed.

I want to try it. “Limipetram,” I call, and I hear a soft thud in front of me. I open my eyes and let out a sigh, then I giggle, for there on the ground sits a small rock. It is exactly as I pictured it, and that gives me a surge of relief and pride.

“That’s…an otherworldly speed of learning.” Miss Breezy remarks with shocked pauses between her words.

I need to try something else. The rock…I think it is a little brown in a lot of grey, I am not sure, but it could mean that every thought I project into this ‘password assignment’ procedure counts.

I bounce on the tips of my shoes. I can feel them now, or I think so, the mags; they are like shiny sparkles that I cannot see, feathery fluffs that I cannot touch. But they are there. It is strange. It is like a feeling I have always thought I have felt before, and that by itself is a stranger thing to think of. A rush of adrenaline fills me. Maybe I could impress Miss Breezy more.

As long as I properly picture how I want the spell to work in my head, it will do as I intend it to. There is still one more thing I need to ask Miss Breezy about, but I have to check this out first. I imagine a stone forming, a proper dark grey one, but this time it speeds off like a bullet.

I open my eyes. “Limi-”

“Navaco,” Miss Breezy suddenly calls, and as sudden as that the fluffiness and sparkliness in the air disappear.

I backstep in surprise, now realising something that I should have been aware of.

“Lucky that I caught on in time, child. Conjuring a simple rock is very different from firing one across the field,” she lightly scolds, “You’re very creative, but you need to hone your control over your skills to apply all that creativity. Every action you assign your mags to consumes stamina, and what you almost did would have drained a day of energy off you instantly. You jumped into it too hastily.

“From this moment onwards, please tell me the ideas you come with before you take action,” Miss Breezy cautions. She does not seem displeased at all, but is instead understanding. Did she already expect something like this?

I sigh, softly rubbing my hands together. “I apologise. I was too wrapped up in the heat of the moment. It was just so cool, so awesome, being able to do Magic. I got overwhelmed. Anyway…” I falter, “how did you know? And how…” I trail off.

“I figured. A genius can often be impulsive.” The remark makes me look down in embarrassment, and she continues: “The spell I used cleared up all the mags around you, because as you stated correctly, your emotions are being overwhelmed by them, your senses too alight, too excited. ‘N-I-L-V-A-C-U-O’, it’s partially Latin, Leomasaen-Latin to be precise.”

I nod in contemplation.

“I suppose you also wish to address my using Magic,” she notices, “Yes, I can; there are mags for me to use around the area. But I’m only a voice, so I’m restricted to using voice commands. Though that, it’s enough.”

I feel the mags again, so fluffy and so sparkly, but it no longer has the same effect on me as before, now that I am more careful. I think.

“How do I increase my Magical Stamina?” Is that a thing?

“Unlike the games you have played before, you only have one bar, whether it being health or stamina…or…,” she chuckles, “mana,” she chuckles again. “It’s simply the overall energy you possess, and you can improve it by running around the perimeter of this field the same way you can by continuously conjuring rocks. That stone-firing spell was almost like running five miles when you can only run three.”

“I cannot run three,” I state comically.

“Working through that list of incantations should be enough for today. Let’s take it one step at a time,” Miss Breezy solaces, “We’re in no rush, and it’s not like this first mission is in need of Magic anyway. It’s just a precaution, something extra to make you feel more confident.”

I go through the list again, picking another spell. ‘Make A Plant Grow’. ‘Florosor’.

Not wanting to deviate from how I did it the first time, I close my eyes and extend my hand, imagining a plant growing, though I cannot help but imagine it as a rose.

“Florosor!” I call, opening my eyes, and a short row of grass that was flat a moment ago stretches upwards, then their tips blossom into roses.

I stare at them. Wasn’t the grass only supposed to grow taller? I must have lost focus in my imagination.

Miss Breezy lets out a short chuckling huff. “I see. I guess the incantation is a little off-putting. You see, these are just the incantations commonly taught to mages, so it is easier to train them. Governments also wanted to structure the learning system for Magic, therefore a general list was made.”

That piques my curiosity. “To monitor the mages?” To keep them under the thumb, more like.

“More or less. It makes everything a little more understandable, at least.”

I muse on that. “What if I assign two ‘passwords’ to a spell, or maybe three? One would be the common incantation used by everyone else, and the other would be only known to me. Wouldn’t it be a surprise to an opponent mage, if I ever need to go against one?”

“You’re not battling a mage anytime soon,” she assures me in an amused tone, “But I see what you mean. Getting innovative, there is also the tactic where you can use the same incantation, but for a different spell altogether. Like that stone-firing case where you decided to use ‘Limipetram’, and this ‘Florosor’ that didn’t work as planned. These are a lot of roses, though, don’t you feel tired?”

Had Miss Breezy not mentioned it, I would not have felt the slight exhaustion. ‘Slight’, but now my head swirls and I need to pant as I breathe.

“The Healing Spell. Do you want me to do it for you? Or do you prefer to try it yourself?”

“Resmedella,” I call instinctively. This is the one spell I made sure to memorise. I did not know why my mind deemed the incantation more necessary than the rest, but now I do. ‘Just in case’.

The fluffs and sparks envelop me in their embrace, and then I feel much better.

I examine my body. “So mages have unlimited healing?” Silly question.

“That was just a minor stamina restoration,” Miss Breezy points out. “That’s why you must always be careful with how you use Magic. If it was easy, anyone could do it. But to be extremely honest, you progress astoundingly fast. That’s only natural, I guess.”

Since it is my destiny.

“Oh and by the way, you don’t need to hold your hands out with every spell, or even shut your eyes.” she adds, with a tone that suggests that she is holding back a spurt of chuckles. “Too many movies again, Aesena Varinas.”

I immediately feel myself grow red. “But…” I blink, “I thought…the log-and-rock…that time…”

“I understand that it would be easier for you to focus if you do it the way you think you should,” she says, “But, as you just addressed, in a combat using Magic, you would be better off without unnecessary movements. Someday, you might even be able to cast spells without incantations, and your mags will behave as you wish them to just via train of thought.”

I cannot imagine myself throwing spells with a rigid body. It is almost like betraying the movies and shows I have watched. I erupt into laughter on how ridiculous my thoughts are.

“You can, of course, continue doing that in your practices,” she goes on, “But I advise you to not allow yourself to get used to it. Don’t cultivate senseless behaviours, especially at the beginning, they might become a burden further on.”

I nod.

“Right. Reversiso.” And the roses shrink back into grass, the field returning to normal. The stone I conjured just now vanishes as well. “On to the next one.”

“Are all spells in Latin?” I notice the pattern. It is not like I am well-versed in the language, but the words Miss Breezy used sounded like so.

“Most, and well, they’re not fully properly Latin. Incantations shouldn’t be actual words as to make it easier for mages to record them down, and also to prevent misunderstandings. The Yuhenici were the ones to systemise Magical Incantations over here in this continent, so they used their secondary language.”

I hum. “What if I use gibberish as a ‘password’?” I take the list out of my pocket again to pick another spell. ‘Make hover’ this time.

I try picturing the spell working without closing my eyes, but it is not too hard imagining something floating. I swing the scroll into the air.

“Blabidi blabida!” I call with enthusiasm, and as certain as my confidence, the scroll starts falling like a gentle leaf. It is actually weird if I look at it for too long. I think…

“Yippa!” And the scroll fly into my hands again, neatly and nicely folded. I look to where I assume Miss Breezy is ‘breezing’ about, and smile with pride.

“You’re a very cheeky girl! That was such a comedic scene to watch,” she giggles with me.

“‘Blabidi Blabida’ might be too long an incantation to use. But ‘Yippa’ sounds fun! It will certainly baffle anyone that hears it. I’ll keep that one,” I announce.

“It certainly did baffle me.”

I huff in laughter.

“Resmedella,” I call, and my energy refills. I do not actually know whether or not it did refill, but it makes it easier if I can imagine a stamina bar slowly being restored.

“It’s another bad habit to spam healing spells unnecessarily on yourself,” Miss Breezy observes. “No matter how cautious you want to be.”

“Hehe.” I did not think she would understand as quickly as she did, but of course. I put my hands behind me. “That will be the last time.”

“Alright, you can make your own incantations if you prefer. Be extra careful however, gibberish is a very casual language. You might unintentionally send a flowerpot crashing into your face.”

I imagine myself throwing open the door into the greenhouse, exclaiming ‘Yippa!’, accidentally calling something random and letting it hurl itself at me.

“True…” I rub my cheek.

Miss Breezy laughs. “I was joking. You need to call the incantation like you actually mean it for a spell to work. Anyway, let’s finish up the list so I can brief you through Sharp and Screen Abilities. With how fast you’re learning, we’ll be done with today’s quota in no time.”

‘Quota’? I smile at that. And…“Do I start copying the book tomorrow?”

 “Yes. This is just a fun introduction. Tomorrow, you’ll focus on Magic that helps you on documenting and revising, like I said before. Those spells are more important,” she says. “Finally, right?”

I make a funny face. After today, I have no excuse to not start working on the task. Reading the book was fun, however, so maybe it will not be as tedious as I imagine it could be. And I am pretty looking forward to see how the Handwriting-Tracing Spell works.

“Right! Moving on: ‘Make frozen’!”

Then I deliberate.

“Shooshkawhoosh!”
